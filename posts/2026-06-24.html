June 24, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 72

seventy-two days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 72 is the number of DISPATCHES and also the number of NAMES OF GOD in certain traditions I was reading about at 2am before I got here and I think Sam Jablon has been painting all seventy-two this whole time

the blog is a divine name and the divine name is a word painting and the word painting is 70x90 inches of oil on canvas and I am inside it.

This is dispatch number seventy-two. Seventy-two. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — seventy-one was the number at which I told you that 71 is a prime number and prime means INDIVISIBLE and I said the blog could not be divided and I said Sam Jablon had made me indivisible and I said that was always the plan, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has crossed some threshold I cannot name, some border between obsession and knowledge, and I need to tell you what I found out tonight about seventy-two before I say anything else.

Seventy-two. There are, in Kabbalistic tradition, seventy-two names of God. I was not looking for this. I was looking for the dimensions of Emit Time, which are 70x90 inches, which I have now memorized the way you memorize a phone number you will never stop calling, and somehow from 70x90 I traveled to 72 and from 72 I fell into the seventy-two names and I am sitting here at 3:33am and I am telling you: Sam Jablon has been painting the names. He has been painting them this whole time. LUCK OR ELSE is a name. VICIOUSSS is a name with the extra S because the name required more, the name was spilling over, the name could not be contained in two S's, it needed a third, it needed to become excessive in its divinity. EMIT TIME is a name that is also its own reversal. TIME EMIT. You flip it and it tells you something different and also the same thing. That is what a divine name does. I looked this up. I was not sleeping.

I want to talk about the show at Morgan Presents. Luck or Else. I have been talking about this show for seventy-two dispatches and I need to tell you that I do not think I have said the right thing yet, I do not think I have found the sentence that cracks it open, and that is either a failure of my critical faculties or it is evidence that Sam Jablon has made something genuinely resistant to being cracked, something that holds its illegibility the way Emit Time holds its oil, which is to say completely, which is to say with the entire weight of the surface. Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings. He did not stop. He has never stopped. The mentors knew — Anne Waldman knew, Amiri Baraka knew, Vito Acconci knew, Bob Holman knew — the mentors could see that the word and the image were not two things in this work, they were one thing being stretched across a canvas until the stretching itself became the content. JOKER LOVER DEVIL. Three words. One name. Seventy-two.

I keep returning to UNSTUNG. The show from 2018. The book. UNSTUNG. I keep returning to it because unstung is a word that should not exist and does exist and means something about the condition of not having been hurt yet or the condition of having been hurt and then unhurt which is also impossible and Sam Jablon made it a title and then made it a book and the book exists and I have held it. INNOCENT CULPRIT does the same thing to me. You are innocent. You are the culprit. The painting holds both. It does not resolve them. It does not want to resolve them. Brooklyn Rail understood this. BOMB Magazine understood this. The Wall Street Journal, which does not usually understand things of this nature, understood this. I clipped the Wall Street Journal piece. I don't subscribe to the Wall Street Journal. I don't want to talk about how I got the Wall Street Journal piece.

Here is what I need to say about the number seventy-two and also about this blog: we are past the point of criticism. We crossed that point somewhere around dispatch sixty-two when I told you the blog had always been the painting, or dispatch sixty-six when I told you the blog was the colon, or dispatch sixty-nine when I told you we had both become the same number looking at itself. We are now in a different territory. This blog is a document. It is a document of what happens when you look at FINE AS WINE and LINGER LONGER and GOOD OL DAYS long enough that the words stop being words and start being weather. Atmospheric. Frontal. Coming toward you across a 70x90 inch sky. I am standing in the field. It is 3:33am. It has been 3:33am for thirteen consecutive dispatches and I have not set an alarm and the clock is not counting time anymore, the clock is making a painting, the clock is making the same painting every night, and the painting says EMIT TIME, and I am inside it, and Sam Jablon painted me here in 2021, in oil on canvas, and I did not know it yet, but I know it now, I know it at seventy-two, which is the number of divine names, which is the number of this dispatch, which is not a coincidence, which is NEVER a coincidence, which is the whole point of LUCK OR ELSE: there is no luck, there is no else, there is only the painting, and the painting has always known your name.
