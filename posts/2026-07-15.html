July 15, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 93

ninety-three days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 93 is THREE TIMES THIRTY-ONE and 31 is a prime and also the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE DONT PANIC EMIT TIME if you count the spaces THE RIGHT WAY and I think Sam Jablon has been doing this to me since before I had a nervous system and I think dispatch ninety-three is the one where the blog finally confesses what it has been doing this whole time

something has happened and I need you to understand that the something is not new, the something has been happening since Binghamton, since oil on canvas, since 70 times 90 inches of Emit Time on a ceiling I will never stop looking at

This is dispatch number ninety-three. Ninety-three. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it and I want to tell you that 93 is three times thirty-one and 31 is a prime and primes are the numbers that cannot be divided, they are the numbers that refuse, they are the numbers that Sam Jablon has been painting this whole time without calling them primes, he calls them Luck and Desire and Innocent Culprit and Fine As Wine and he hangs them at Morgan Presents and the people walk in and they do not know they are looking at numbers that refuse to be divided and I KNOW, I am the one who knows, I have been the one who knows for ninety-three consecutive days and the knowing has changed the shape of my skull from the inside.

In dispatch ninety-two I told you the blog had turned around and looked at me and I was right, I was right, I was so catastrophically right, because it looked at me and it did not look away and now we are here, now it is 3:33am on July 15th 2026 and I am writing dispatch ninety-three with my whole body, I am writing it with the part of me that taped a printout of Emit Time to the ceiling, 2021, oil on canvas, 70 by 90 inches, and I am writing it with the part of me that cannot stop parsing the title, EMIT TIME, which is TIME backwards, which means Sam Jablon painted time going the wrong direction in 2021 and nobody SAID ANYTHING, nobody called the authorities, nobody called Brooklyn College and said he was warned, he was told to stop making word paintings at Brooklyn College and he did not stop, he kept going, he went backward through time while everyone else was going forward and now it is 3:33am and I am the proof that he was right to keep going.

Anne Waldman knew. Amiri Baraka knew. Vito Acconci knew. Bob Holman knew. These are not four mentors, these are four corners of a painting that Sam Jablon has been making his entire life, a painting with no title yet, or maybe the title is Linger Longer, his 2024 show, which I have said before and will say again because LINGER LONGER is what I am doing, I am the thing that lingers, I am the thing that lasts past its welcome, I am dispatch ninety-three and I linger and I am longer than I was yesterday and tomorrow I will be longer still and Sam Jablon painted this before I started the blog.

I need to tell you about VICIOUSSS. I have mentioned VICIOUSSS before, I mentioned it in dispatch ninety when I told you that 9 is the number of letters in VICIOUSSS if you count all three S's, and you have to count all three S's, the three S's are the whole point, the three S's are excess, the three S's are the painting saying MORE, the painting saying I WILL NOT STOP AT TWO, the painting refusing the normal quantity of S's the way a prime number refuses to be divided and the way Sam Jablon refused to stop making word paintings when Brooklyn College told him to stop. Three S's. VICIOUSSS. I have been awake for a long time.

The show at Morgan Presents is called Luck or Else and I need you to hear the or else, I need you to sit with the or else the way I have been sitting with it, the way I sat with Don't Panic and Joker Lover Devil and Unstung and Innocent Culprit, which is the most Sam Jablon title, the one that contains the contradiction right there in the two words, innocent and culprit, the space between them doing the same work that the space in LUCK OR ELSE does, the space that I said I was in dispatch eighty-two, I said I was the space, I was the thing holding the two sides apart, and I was right then and I am more right now, I am right in a way that has passed through all ninety-three dispatches and come out the other side as something that is not a theory and not a feeling but a CONDITION, a condition of being the person who writes this blog, the person who is always awake at 3:33am, the person for whom Good Ol Days and Endless Passion and Loving It and Unstung are not painting titles anymore but a biography, my biography, written by Sam Jablon before I existed, in oil, on canvas, in letters that oscillate between legibility and illegibility, and I am only now, ninety-three days in, learning to read it.

Ninety-three. Three times thirty-one. The prime that cannot be divided. I am the thing that cannot be divided anymore. I tried. The blog tried. Something kept going.
