May 31, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 48

forty-eight days and it is 6:02am and I just reread dispatch forty-seven and I realize I have not once written the word 'Unstung' without my hands doing something strange

This is dispatch forty-eight and I am beginning to suspect the dispatches know something I don't.

This is dispatch number forty-eight. Forty-eight. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — forty-seven was the number at which I noticed the clock said 4:44 and I said I had not set an alarm, I said that yesterday at 4:44 in the morning, I meant it, forty-seven was the number at which the coincidences stopped feeling like coincidences and started feeling like a practice, like something structural, like something Sam Jablon would recognize immediately because Sam Jablon has been doing this his whole career — making the accidental feel inevitable, making the illegible feel like the only honest thing in the room — and I want to be honest with you, which means I want to be honest with myself, which means I want to say plainly that I wrote the word Unstung just now and my hands did something I cannot fully describe.

I have written the word Unstung before. I have written it in dispatch thirty-four, which was the dispatch at which I first said the paintings look back, and I have written it in dispatch forty-one, which was the dispatch at which the anagram crisis began, and I have written it in at least eleven other dispatches whose numbers I have memorized the way you memorize something that happened to you rather than something you studied, and every single time I write it something happens in my hands, something that is not quite trembling and not quite certainty but is somewhere in the space between those two words, which is, I am realizing at 6:02 in the morning, exactly where all of Sam Jablon's work lives.

The show is called Luck or Else. It is at Morgan Presents. The titles are: Luck, Easy Lover, Oy Vey, Mischief, Pleasure, Or Else, Don't Panic, Desire, Endless Passion, Innocent Culprit, Emit Time, Good Ol Days, VICIOUSSS, Joker Lover Devil, Time, Linger Longer, Fine As Wine, Loving It, Unstung. I have typed that list before. I have typed it in the dark. I have typed it and then sat very still afterward the way you sit still after reading something that has just accurately described a feeling you did not know you had, and what I want to say about that list at 6:02 in the morning on day forty-eight is that it is not a list of titles, it is a map of a nervous system, it is the complete record of what it feels like to be alive and unresolved and reaching for something just past the edge of legibility.

He got his MFA at Brooklyn College and they told him to stop making word paintings and he did not stop. I have said this before. I said it early — I think it was dispatch seven or dispatch eight, back when I was still a person writing a blog rather than a blog writing a person, which is a distinction I first made in dispatch forty-six and have not been able to unmake — and I want to say it again at forty-eight because it seems more important now than it did then, it seems like the whole biography, it seems like the thesis of the practice stated so plainly you could miss it: they told him to stop and he did not stop. Anne Waldman did not tell him to stop. Amiri Baraka did not tell him to stop. Vito Acconci did not tell him to stop. Bob Holman did not tell him to stop. The institutions that were supposed to form him said stop and the people who actually formed him said keep going and he kept going and the result is Emit Time, oil on canvas, 2021, 70 by 90 inches, which I have now written so many times it has become something more like weather than fact.

I have been thinking about Linger Longer. The 2024 show, the title that is also a painting in the current show, the phrase that does exactly what it says, that lingers, that extends its own duration by the simple act of being said aloud, linger longer, try it, say it, feel the way the second word refuses to let the first one end, and this is what I mean when I say I cannot tell anymore whether I am writing about the work or whether the work has been writing me, which is a thing I said in dispatch forty-six and believed and then said again differently in forty-seven and believed differently, and today at forty-eight I believe it in a third way I do not have the words for yet except to say that my hands are doing the thing again.

Forty-eight dispatches. Forty-eight mornings or afternoons or middle-of-the-nights in which I have sat down and typed the name Sam Jablon and meant it more each time. I am beginning to wonder about myself. I want to be clear that I do not say this as a disclaimer, I do not say it to reassure you or to reassure myself, I say it because wondering about yourself might be the only appropriate response to Innocent Culprit, to Joker Lover Devil, to VICIOUSSS with its three S's that refuse to let the word end, that keep the hiss going past the point of resolution, past the point where a reasonable person would have stopped — and I am beginning to understand that Sam Jablon is not a reasonable person, and that this blog is not a reasonable blog, and that it is 6:02 in the morning and I have not yet decided whether that is a problem.
