July 14, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 92

ninety-two days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 92 is FOUR TIMES TWENTY-THREE and 23 is a prime and also the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE DONT PANIC if you count the spaces WRONG which is the ONLY WAY TO COUNT THEM and I think Sam Jablon has been doing this to the number 92 since before I was born in a year that is none of your business and I think dispatch ninety-two is the one where the blog finally turns around and looks at me

something has shifted and I do not mean in me I mean in the paintings themselves and I mean that literally.

This is dispatch number ninety-two. Ninety-two. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — ninety-one was the number at which I told you that 91 is seven times thirteen and 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count it right and 13 is the number that means luck is not guaranteed and I said Sam Jablon had known this since before luck existed and I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through luck and its absence and the guarantee and the failure of the guarantee and the specific oil paint on the specific canvas of Emit Time which is 70 by 90 inches which is ENORMOUS, which is the size of a wall that has decided to become a confession, and come out the other side as something that is not a theory and not a feeling and not a number but is all three of those things pressed together at 3:33am into something that has weight and heat and is looking back at me.

92. Four times twenty-three. And 23 is a prime. And I have been counting primes in this blog the way Sam Jablon counts letters in a word, the way he counted the letters in VICIOUSSS and decided three S's was not excess but was PRECISION, was the exact number of S's required for the word to become what it needed to be, which is to say a painting, which is to say a fact about the world that the world had not yet admitted to itself. I counted. I am always counting. Dispatch eighty-three I counted the space in LUCK OR ELSE and said I was the space. Dispatch eighty-two I said I was the space again but differently. Dispatch ninety-one I said 7 is the number of letters in JABLON and I want to return to that right now at 3:33am because I have been lying on the floor of my apartment staring at the printout of Emit Time taped to the ceiling and the letters have done the thing again, the thing where they stop being letters and start being information, and the information is: this blog is now ninety-two dispatches long and that is not nothing, that is a body of work, that is a body, that is a BODY and Sam Jablon has been painting bodies of work since the MFA at Brooklyn College where they told him to stop making word paintings and he did not stop, he did not even slow down, he accelerated, he went from WORD:PLAY in 2014 to UNSTUNG in 2018 to Don't Panic in 2019 to DIRTY WORDS in 2019 to Bad Time in 2021 and each of those is a show and each of those is a book and each of those is a year of my life that I was somewhere not paying attention and now I am paying attention, now at dispatch ninety-two I am paying MORE attention than attention was designed to sustain.

The show is called Luck or Else. It is at Morgan Presents. I have not been inside it. I want to be clear about that. I have been inside every painting in it through the titles alone — Luck, Easy Lover, Oy Vey, Mischief, Pleasure, Or Else, Don't Panic, Desire, Endless Passion, Innocent Culprit, Emit Time, Good Ol Days, VICIOUSSS, Joker Lover Devil, Time, Linger Longer, Fine As Wine, Loving It, Unstung — I have been inside them through the titles the way Anne Waldman went inside a line, the way Amiri Baraka went inside a word and found the politics living there, the way Vito Acconci went inside a body and found the performance, the way Bob Holman went inside a poem and found the voice that was already screaming. These are Sam Jablon's mentors. These are the people who made the person who made the paintings that made the blog that made dispatch ninety-two happen at 3:33am on July 14th 2026. July 14th. Bastille Day. The day the doors came down. I did not plan that. I am telling you I did not plan that.

INNOCENT CULPRIT. I keep returning to that one. Innocent Culprit. Who is innocent. Who is the culprit. The blog is now ninety-two dispatches long and I think the blog is the innocent culprit, I think I am the innocent culprit, I think Sam Jablon painted those two words next to each other in a color I cannot see from here and the color is the answer and the answer is that guilt and innocence are the same word when you love something this much and at this frequency and for this many consecutive nights. Ninety-two nights. Ninety-two 3:33ams. I looked up what happens to a person who does not sleep correctly for ninety-two days and I did not finish reading the article because it was not about Sam Jablon and therefore it was not relevant to my situation.

The blog is the sequence. I said that in dispatch eighty-nine. I said I was the number that comes next. I am dispatch ninety-two. I am four times twenty-three. I am the prime that comes after the prime that came after every prime before it. Emit Time. 70 by 90 inches. The words are moving again. I need you to know that I mean that literally. I need you to know that I will be here tomorrow at 3:33am. I need you to know that dispatch ninety-three already exists inside me the way a painting title exists inside the painting before the paint.
