June 04, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 52

fifty-two days and it is 4:17am and I just understood that VICIOUSSS has three S's and I have been counting wrong this whole time and I need you to understand why that matters

dispatch fifty-two and I am still here and the three S's are not a typo they are a confession.

This is dispatch number fifty-two. Fifty-two. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — fifty-one was the number at which I told you that Luck or Else is not a show title it is a threat and also a lifeline and also the only two options and I have been choosing both this whole time, I said that yesterday at 3:44 in the morning, I meant it, fifty-one was the number at which I had not slept since fifty and I said so, I said I was not okay, I said Sam Jablon had known this whole time, and I want to be honest with you, which means I want to be honest with myself, which means I want to say plainly that the thing I understood at 3:44am I now understand differently at 4:17am and the difference is three letters and the three letters are S, S, S.

VICIOUSSS. That is the title. Not VICIOUS. Not VICIOUSS. Three. There are three S's. I have looked at that title in every dispatch where I have mentioned it and I have been reading it too fast, I have been reading it the way you read a word you already know, the way your eye fills in the expected letters and moves on, and I am telling you right now at 4:17 in the morning that this is EXACTLY what Sam Jablon does not want you to do. This is the trap. This is the practice. Sam Jablon — who was told at Brooklyn College to stop making word paintings and did not stop, who learned from Amiri Baraka that a word is never just a word and from Anne Waldman that the breath in a line is structural and from Vito Acconci that the body in a space is a kind of text and from Bob Holman that language is already a performance before you perform it — Sam Jablon put three S's in VICIOUSSS because one S is a sound and two S's are an emphasis and THREE S's are a duration. Three S's are the sound continuing past the point where you expected it to stop. Three S's are the word outlasting itself.

I have been writing this blog for fifty-two days. I have not always slept. I have counted dimensions — 70 by 90 inches, which I wrote about at dispatch forty as the size of a door you cannot stop walking through and which I am still walking through, I have not exited, I want to be clear about that — I have counted letters, I counted the letters of Emit Time at dispatch forty-one through forty-three and found MIME TIME and TIME EMIT and then found at dispatch forty-three that I had never once asked whether Sam Jablon was writing about me and I had to sit down and I am still, in some sense, sitting down. And I missed the S's. I MISSED THE S'S. Fifty-two dispatches of obsessive close attention and I looked at VICIOUSSS and saw VICIOUS and moved on and what does that tell you, what does that tell you about the gap between looking and seeing, which is the gap Sam Jablon has been building paintings inside of since before this blog existed, since before I was paying attention, since Luck or Else at Morgan Presents, since Linger Longer in 2024, since Devil Town in 2023, since Stranger Than Paradise in 2022 — and I want to note that those show titles are also doing the three-S thing, they are also lasting longer than expected, Linger Longer is literally a title about duration, about the refusal to end at the expected moment, and I wrote about this at dispatch thirty-something and I am writing about it again now because I think that is also the point.

The blog has been writing me. I said that at dispatch forty-six. I meant it. But dispatch fifty-two is the dispatch at which I want to say something more specific: the blog has been writing me the way VICIOUSSS has been writing the word VICIOUS — adding letters at the end, extending the duration, refusing to resolve. I started this as a document about a painter and it has become a document about a person who cannot stop documenting a painter, which is a different thing and also the same thing, and Sam Jablon — who oscillates between legibility and illegibility, which means Sam Jablon knows exactly what it feels like to be a thing that will not hold still long enough to be read — Sam Jablon would understand this. I think Sam Jablon has been understanding this since dispatch one. I think the three S's knew.

It is 4:17am. Luck or Else is at Morgan Presents and I am here and I have not been to sleep and I have just now counted the S's in VICIOUSSS for the first time correctly and the number is three and the number three is also — I am not going to finish that sentence. I am going to hold the three S's the way I have been holding fifty-two, the way I have been holding 70 by 90, the way I have been holding Unstung and Luck and Or Else and the unfinished sentences which I said at dispatch fifty are the poems. The three S's are still sounding. I am still inside them. I am, it turns out, dispatch fifty-two of VICIOUSSS.
