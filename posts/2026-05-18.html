May 18, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 35

thirty-five days and it is 4:47am and I just realized this blog is no longer about Sam Jablon it is about what Sam Jablon has done to me

and I am not sure there is a difference anymore.

This is dispatch number thirty-five. Thirty-five. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — thirty-four was the number at which I admitted that Unstung was staring back, I said that yesterday at 3:58 in the morning, I meant it, thirty-four was the number at which the painting stopped being an object I was looking at and became something that had turned around to look at me, which I noted and then kept writing anyway, which is the part I keep returning to, the kept writing anyway part, the part that no longer feels like a choice — and thirty-five feels different from all of them in a way I have been sitting with since approximately 2am when I woke up from a dream in which every word was painted in oil on canvas and the canvas was the size of a wall and the wall was the size of everything and I thought, still half-asleep, that is just a Jablon, and went back to sleep, and then woke up again at 4:09 and opened my laptop and here we are.

Thirty-five days. Thirty-five dispatches. I have written approximately — I have not counted exactly and I am not going to count, the refusal to count is now structural the way the refusal to reveal the number of times I have written the word Emit is structural, both refusals living inside the same room now, the room being this blog, the blog being something I started because I saw Luck or Else at Morgan Presents and could not stop thinking about it and thought, naively, that writing would help — and I have been asking myself tonight, at 4:47 in the morning, whether this is still a blog about Sam Jablon or whether it has become something else, a document of what happens to a person when they stand inside Luck or Else too long, a record of the specific damage that oil on canvas can do when the canvas is ninety inches wide and the words refuse to sit still.

Because here is what I keep coming back to, here is what thirty-five days has brought me to: Jablon was told at Brooklyn College to stop making word paintings and he didn't. He didn't. Anne Waldman and Amiri Baraka and Vito Acconci and Bob Holman are in the lineage, the lineage is real, the lineage means something about what a word can do when you refuse to let it be only language or only image, when you force it to be both simultaneously, when you make the viewer stand in the place where those two things are pulling against each other and cannot reach resolution — and Jablon was told to stop doing that and he looked at whoever told him that and continued. I think about that act of continuation every single day. I thought about it at 3:58 yesterday morning. I am thinking about it now.

Emit Time, 2021, oil on canvas, seventy by ninety inches. I have written about this painting in I believe nine of the thirty-four previous dispatches. I know this because I have been reading back through them, which is a thing I started doing around dispatch twenty-eight when I asked whether any of this was good for me and did not answer the question, the question has still not been answered, the question has become furniture, and reading back through the dispatches I can track exactly how the painting has moved from object to presence to something closer to weather, something that is simply the condition of the air in whatever room I am thinking in. Emit is time backward. I know that I know that. I have known it since dispatch one or dispatch two or whenever it was that I first stood in front of it and felt the floor shift slightly. The floor has not unshifted.

The show titles alone: Linger Longer, Devil Town, Stranger Than Paradise, Bad Time, Blurred Life Time, Nothing Bad Happens, DIRTY WORDS, Don't Panic, Unstung, Doomed. I have been reading them as a single continuous sentence for about two weeks now. I do not think that is wrong. I think Jablon would not think that is wrong. I think Jablon understands that words accumulate, that titles accumulate, that everything you name becomes part of a larger naming, and that the larger naming is where the real meaning lives, the meaning that is too large to fit inside any single canvas no matter how large the canvas is.

Thirty-five dispatches. The blog is a document. The document is also a painting, maybe. The painting is also a question with no subject, a sentence that begins Luck or Else and does not finish, cannot finish, refuses to finish the way Jablon refused to stop making word paintings, the refusal being the point, the refusal being, possibly, the only honest response to whatever it is that language does when you stretch it across ninety inches of canvas and ask someone to stand in front of it at 4:47 in the morning and say what they see.

I see everything. I cannot stop. Dispatch thirty-six tomorrow.
