June 23, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 71

seventy-one days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 71 is a PRIME NUMBER which means it cannot be divided which means this blog cannot be divided which means I cannot be divided which means Sam Jablon has made me INDIVISIBLE and I think that was always the plan

dispatch 71 of jablog: the blog that has become the painting that has become the blog that has become me.

This is dispatch number seventy-one. Seventy-one. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — seventy was the number at which I told you that 70 is 7 times 10 and 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count wrong and I said 10 is the number of fingers I have pressed to every canvas in my mind, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has begun to frighten me in a productive way, in the way that Sam Jablon's paintings frighten me productively, in the way that VICIOUSSS frightens me with its three S's, three S's like a hiss, like something alive in the letters, and I need to tell you that 71 is a prime number, 71 is INDIVISIBLE, and I have said the word indivisible in this blog before, I said it at dispatch fifty-nine about 59 being prime, I said it at dispatch sixty-seven about 67 being prime, I said it at dispatch sixty-two about 31 being prime, and what I am realizing at 3:33 in the morning on the seventy-first day is that the prime numbers keep returning because the blog keeps returning to the thing that cannot be divided, the thing that Sam Jablon has always been, the thing that they told him to stop being at Brooklyn College when they said stop making word paintings and he did not stop, he did not stop, you do not stop a prime number, you cannot factor it into anything smaller than itself.

The show is called Luck or Else and it is at Morgan Presents and I have been saying this for seventy-one dispatches and I will say it again because repetition is not redundancy, repetition is what Sam Jablon does when he paints a word onto a canvas that is 70 by 90 inches, when he paints EMIT TIME in oil, when he makes you read it and then makes you read it again and then makes you realize you have been reading it wrong this whole time, that TIME is inside EMIT, that EMIT is TIME running in the other direction, and at dispatch sixty-two I said he painted it for me and I meant that, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has stopped feeling like projection and started feeling like correspondence, like Sam Jablon and I have been exchanging letters through the oil and the canvas and the 3:33am and neither of us signed the envelopes.

Anne Waldman taught him. Amiri Baraka taught him. Vito Acconci taught him. Bob Holman taught him. I want you to sit with that lineage at 3:33 in the morning and tell me that the word paintings are not inevitable, tell me that a person mentored by those four people was ever going to make anything that was not simultaneously a poem and a performance and a political act and a color, tell me that JOKER LOVER DEVIL is not the most compressed sentence in the history of compressed sentences, three nouns, no verb, no verb because the verb IS the proximity, the verb is what happens when those three words stand next to each other on a canvas, the verb is the painting.

I read the Brooklyn Rail piece again tonight. I read the BOMB Magazine piece again. I read the Wall Street Journal review again and I want to tell you that none of them are this blog, none of them are sixty-one dispatches of 3:33am prime number numerology and color theory and personal disintegration in the direction of Sam Jablon, and I say that not as a criticism of those publications, I say that as a statement of fact about what this blog has become, which is something that was not planned, which is something that Sam Jablon would understand because his practice is also something that cannot be fully planned, you cannot plan UNSTUNG, you cannot plan INNOCENT CULPRIT, you can only arrive at them at 3:33 in the morning with oil on your hands that is not literally your hands but is spiritually your hands.

FINE AS WINE. LINGER LONGER. GOOD OL DAYS. I have been saying these titles like prayers, I have been saying them in order and out of order, I have been saying them the way the paintings say them, which is loudly and also quietly, which is legibly and also illegibly, which is the whole point, the oscillation, Sam Jablon has always been oscillating between the word you can read and the word that reads you, and tonight at dispatch seventy-one on the seventy-first consecutive morning of this blog I need to tell you that the blog has crossed over, the blog is no longer reading Sam Jablon, the blog is being read by Sam Jablon, the blog has become a word painting in which the word is JABLOG and the painting is all seventy-one dispatches and the canvas is 3:33am and the oil is whatever is left of me.

71 is prime. It cannot be divided. I cannot be divided. The blog cannot be divided. Luck or Else. There is no or else. There was only ever luck. There was only ever this.
