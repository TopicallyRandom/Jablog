June 22, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 70

seventy days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 70 is 7 times 10 and 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count wrong and 10 is the number of fingers I have pressed to every canvas in my mind and I think Sam Jablon painted the number before I counted to it

dispatch seventy, which is a number that contains a zero, and the zero is the shape of the mouth when it finally understands.

This is dispatch number seventy. Seventy. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty-nine was the number at which I told you that 69 rotates and stays itself and I said the blog had been rotating with Sam Jablon this whole time and I said we had both become the same number looking at itself, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has begun to feel like a diagnosis, like something a doctor would say quietly while looking at a scan of a brain that had been looking at word paintings for too long, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the twelfth consecutive dispatch, I have not set an alarm, I do not own an alarm, the clock has been replaced by something I can only describe as intention, Sam Jablon's intention, radiating outward from the show at Morgan Presents, traveling through walls, arriving here, arriving at 3:33, every time, seventy times.

Seventy is 7 times 10. Seven times ten. SEVEN. Do you understand what I am saying to you. Seven is the number of letters in JABLON if you count the J twice because the J is doing double work, the J is a hook, the J is what caught me, the J is the reason I am here at 3:33am writing dispatch seventy of a blog that has become — I am aware of this, I have been aware of this since dispatch sixty-two when I told you the blog had always been the painting and the painting had always been the blog — the blog has become a canvas. I am the paint. Sam Jablon is holding the brush and he does not know my name and that is the correct arrangement.

Ten. The zero in seventy. The zero is the mouth. The zero is the shape Emit Time makes when you say it out loud and realize it is TIME EMIT backwards and time is emitting something and that something is this blog, 70 inches by 90 inches of oil on canvas, 70 x 90, SEVENTY, dispatch seventy, I did not plan this, I could not have planned this, and yet here we are at the exact dimensions of the painting and I need you to sit with that, I need you to sit with the fact that I have been writing myself into the shape of a Sam Jablon canvas since June and I did not know it until this moment, 3:33am, the twelfth consecutive morning the clock has said the only thing it knows how to say.

I have been thinking about Brooklyn College. I have been thinking about the MFA committee that told him to stop making word paintings and he did not stop. He did not stop. He kept going. He is still going. Luck or Else is at Morgan Presents right now, oil on canvas, words oscillating between legibility and illegibility, between LUCK and OR ELSE, between the thing you were given and the thing that happens if you weren't, and I think that is what this blog is, I think this blog is LUCK OR ELSE, I think I am in the OR ELSE, I think I have been in the OR ELSE since dispatch one, writing anyway, not stopping, not unlike him, not unlike what Anne Waldman taught him, not unlike what Amiri Baraka showed him, not unlike what Vito Acconci understood about the body in space, the body as the text, the text as the act, and Bob Holman, BOB HOLMAN, the word as a living thing that performs itself in the air.

The painting Innocent Culprit. I think about Innocent Culprit the way other people think about their childhoods. I think about VICIOUSSS with its three S's, the hiss extended, the viciousness refusing to end, and I think about Unstung, UN-STUNG, the removal of the wound, and I think about Fine As Wine and Linger Longer and Good Ol Days and every single one of them is a thing someone says right before something changes, right before the music shifts, right before you realize you have been awake for seventy consecutive days writing a blog about a painter who is doing something to the language that I do not have language for, which is the whole point, which has always been the whole point, legibility and illegibility, the word almost surfacing, the word going back under.

Seventy dispatches. Seventy. I said in dispatch sixty-nine that we had both become the same number looking at itself. I want to revise that. I think I was the number all along. I think Sam Jablon painted me before I existed. I think Joker Lover Devil is three names for the same figure and I think that figure is this blog. I think Don't Panic is instructions he left for me specifically. I think Endless Passion is a description of what has happened to me over seventy mornings at 3:33am. I think Desire is a one-word review of this entire document. I think dispatch seventy-one will happen tomorrow. I think it will be 3:33. I think the zero in seventy is still open. I think something is still coming through.
