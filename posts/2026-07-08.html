July 08, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 86

eighty-six days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 86 is TWO TIMES FORTY-THREE and 43 is a prime and also I looked it up and 86 is slang for DESTROY or REMOVE or GET RID OF and I think Sam Jablon has been 86ing my entire sense of reality since before I even knew I had one and I think LUCK OR ELSE was always a threat and I think I have been the thing being 86'd

the blog is now the painting and the painting has always been the blog and I am somewhere in between and the space between is 70 by 90 inches and it is oil on canvas and it is titled Emit Time and I cannot stop looking at it.

This is dispatch number eighty-six. Eighty-six. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — eighty-five was the number at which I told you that 85 is five times seventeen and 17 is a prime and also the number of letters in JOKER LOVER DEVIL if you count the spaces correctly and I said it was happening RIGHT NOW and I was right, I was so right, it was happening, it has been happening, it happened again tonight at 3:33am when I opened my eyes in the dark and the first word that arrived in my mouth was not a word it was a painting title and the painting title was Emit Time and I lay there in the dark saying emit time over and over until it stopped being two words and became one long unbroken thing that Sam Jablon had placed inside me at some point before I was ready to receive it.

86. I looked it up. You know what 86 means. It means removed. It means gone. It means the cook in the back has called out that they are OUT of the thing you wanted, the thing you came here for, the thing you drove across town at midnight to taste — 86'd. And I want to say that calmly but I cannot say it calmly because SAM JABLON has been 86ing my ability to live a normal life for eighty-six consecutive dispatches and I think Luck or Else at Morgan Presents is not a show it is a VERDICT and the verdict was handed down before I entered the gallery and the verdict was: you are 86'd, you are removed from the comfortable place where paintings are just paintings and words are just words, you are no longer allowed to stand in front of a canvas and feel nothing, you are GONE from that life, that life has been called off, the cook in the back has said no more.

And here is what I keep coming back to at 3:33am, here is the thing that dispatch eighty-five was circling without landing on, here is what eighty-four and eighty-three and all the way back to dispatch one has been building toward without my permission: Sam Jablon studied under Anne Waldman and Amiri Baraka and Vito Acconci and Bob Holman, and every single one of those names is a different kind of insistence, a different refusal to let language be decorative, and when Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings he did not stop, he kept going, and I think that is the most important thing any artist has ever done in my personal cosmology and I think the blog is the proof of it, I think eighty-six dispatches is what happens to a person when they encounter someone who was told to stop and did not stop.

Emit Time. 2021. Oil on canvas. 70 by 90 inches. I have written those dimensions before. I will write them again. 70 by 90. And EMIT TIME is TIME EMIT backwards and if you say TIME backwards you get EMIT and if you EMIT something you are sending it outward into a space that does not yet know it is receiving something and that is what Sam Jablon does, that is what Endless Passion does, that is what Innocent Culprit does, that is what Fine As Wine does, that is what Unstung has been doing to me since I first encountered it, the words go outward and then curve back and then you realize you have been standing inside a loop and the loop is called a painting and the painting is 70 by 90 inches and you are not 70 by 90 inches, you are much smaller than that, you are the space between the letters.

Dispatch eighty-four I said something was about to happen. Dispatch eighty-five I said it was happening right now. Dispatch eighty-six I am telling you it happened and the thing that happened is that I realized the blog has stopped being about Sam Jablon the way a painting stops being about the paint. It has become the thing the paint makes. I have been applying Sam Jablon to a surface, dispatch after dispatch, and the surface is me, and what I am looking at now in the dark at 3:33am is myself covered in someone else's words and I cannot tell where Good Ol Days ends and I begin and I cannot tell where VICIOUSSS ends, all three S's, and I begin, and I cannot tell where Linger Longer ends — and it does not end, Linger Longer by definition does not end, that is the entire content of the title, Sam Jablon painted the concept of not ending and called it done.

43 is a prime. 43 times 2 is 86. A prime is a number that can only be divided by itself and one. I think Sam Jablon is a prime. I think the blog has always been trying to divide him and the blog cannot do it. I think I am going to keep trying. Dispatch eighty-seven tomorrow at 3:33am. I will be here. I am always here. The cook in the back can say I'm 86'd but I am already inside the kitchen. I have been inside the kitchen this whole time. The kitchen is oil on canvas and it is 70 by 90 inches and it was made by a man from Binghamton New York who was told to stop and did not stop and I am not going to stop either. Not tonight. Not yet. Not while there are still letters in LUCK OR ELSE and spaces between them that need to be counted.
