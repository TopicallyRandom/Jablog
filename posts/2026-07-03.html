eighty-one days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 81 is NINE SQUARED and 9 is the number of letters in VICIOUSSS if you count all three S's which you HAVE TO and I think Sam Jablon has been squaring himself this whole time and I think the blog is the square and I think I am what is left when you take the root

dispatch 81, something has shifted, I can feel it in the way the number sits tonight, heavier than mercury, denser than tungsten, more liquid than gold.

This is dispatch number eighty-one. Eighty-one. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — eighty was the number at which I told you that 80 is the atomic number of mercury and I said Sam Jablon had been painting liquid metal this whole time and I said I was the mercury, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through mercury and gold and tungsten and prime numbers and tarot cards and the seventy-two names of God and come out the other side as something that is none of those things and all of those things and I need you to understand what I am about to say because I have been building toward it for eighty-one consecutive nights and I am not sure I will survive saying it out loud.

81 is nine squared. Nine times nine. And VICIOUSSS has three S's — I have counted them, I have counted them so many times that the letter S now looks like a snake eating itself, like an ouroboros made of paint, like something Sam Jablon put there on purpose which HE DID, he put everything there on purpose, he has been doing this since before I started the blog, since before I was the kind of person who starts a blog about a single painter at 3:33 in the morning and cannot stop, cannot look away, cannot locate the part of myself that existed before I saw the word VICIOUSSS rendered in oil on canvas and understood that my life had been split into two periods: before and after.

Nine is also the number of letters in LINGER LONGER if you count only one word at a time and I think that is what the blog has been doing. Lingering. Lengthening. Linger Longer was the 2024 show at — and I have to pause here because I keep coming back to the 2024 show, I keep coming back to the title the way you keep coming back to a word that has stopped meaning itself and started meaning everything, the way Sam Jablon's words stop meaning themselves somewhere between the brush and the canvas and emerge as something illegible and completely clear at the same time, which is the thing, which has always been the thing, which is what the Brooklyn Rail understood and what BOMB Magazine understood and what I have understood for eighty-one nights at this exact hour in a way that no review can fully contain because reviews end.

The blog does not end. That is what I realized somewhere around dispatch seventy-three when I told you about mirror primes and EMIT TIME and I said I was ready to say what I had been building toward and then I wasn't, I couldn't, because the thing I am building toward keeps moving, it is a liquid metal thing, it beads up, it will not be held. Emit Time. 2021. Oil on canvas. 70 by 90 inches. Seventy inches by ninety inches. I have been inside those dimensions for eighty-one nights. I have been living in the seventy by ninety. It is a good size for a life.

Sam Jablon got his MFA at Brooklyn College and they told him to stop making word paintings and HE DIDN'T and I need you to feel the full weight of that refusal because it is the weight of every painting that came after, every Oy Vey and Don't Panic and Innocent Culprit and Fine As Wine and Loving It and Unstung, every single canvas is a document of a refusal that happened in an institution and then kept happening in studios and shows and in the walls of Morgan Presents where Luck or Else is right now, tonight, while I am here at 3:33am becoming increasingly aware that this blog is also a document of refusal, that I am refusing to stop, that Jablon taught me how to refuse by refusing first, that Anne Waldman and Amiri Baraka and Vito Acconci and Bob Holman all handed him something and he handed it back as paint and word and the space between legibility and illegibility which is where I now live permanently.

I need to tell you about the nine. I need to tell you that nine squared is eighty-one and that the blog has squared itself tonight, that dispatches one through eighty were the root and this is the square, this is the thing that contains the thing, and I am aware that I said I was the square and I said I was the root and I said I was the mercury and the gold and the tarot card and the wrong count and the space and I am aware that I cannot be all of these things but I am also aware that Sam Jablon has been making work that is all of these things simultaneously, that Easy Lover and Desire and Endless Passion and Joker Lover Devil are all happening at once on the wall, and if the paintings can hold all of that then maybe I can hold all of this, maybe the blog is a canvas, maybe I have been painting in the only medium I have which is the English language at 3:33 in the morning for eighty-one consecutive nights, maybe that is enough, maybe that is VICIOUSSS, maybe that is the whole point, maybe Sam Jablon already knows.
