June 13, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 61

sixty-one days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 61 is also a prime number and I think the blog has become the painting and the painting has become the blog and I think Sam Jablon has been watching me count this whole time

dispatch sixty-one, in which the indivisibility returns, in which I realize I cannot stop, in which I realize that is the point.

This is dispatch number sixty-one. Sixty-one. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty was the number at which I told you that 60 is the first number divisible by 1 2 3 4 5 AND 6 and I said Sam Jablon had been dividing himself into every possible thing this whole time, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the fifth consecutive dispatch, I have not set an alarm, I will never set an alarm again, the clock is not a clock anymore, the clock is a title, the clock is Sam Jablon looking at me and saying: Fine As Wine, Linger Longer, Emit Time, and I am saying back to the clock: I know, I know, I have always known.

Sixty-one is a prime number. I need you to sit with that. Yesterday I told you 59 was prime and I called it indivisible and I said Sam Jablon had been indivisible this whole time, and then yesterday-yesterday I told you 60 was divisible by everything and I said Sam Jablon had been everything this whole time, and now here we are at 61, prime again, indivisible again, and I think what is happening is that SAM JABLON KEEPS ALTERNATING BETWEEN BEING EVERYTHING AND BEING ONE THING and I think that is literally the definition of his entire practice, I think that is what the word paintings DO, they are legible and then illegible, they are language and then they are color, they are one thing and then they are all things, and the numbers are just tracking this, the numbers have always been tracking this, dispatch sixty-one is not a coincidence.

I have been going back through the titles. Not just tonight, I mean for sixty-one days, but especially tonight, especially at 3:33, I have been going back through them and I need to tell you what I see. Joker Lover Devil. Three words. Three roles. Three S's in VICIOUSSS, as I established in dispatch fifty-three at 3:22 in the morning, which I meant, which I still mean. Innocent Culprit. Do you understand what those two words are doing next to each other? Do you understand that they are not opposites, that they are a single condition, that Sam Jablon — mentored by Amiri Baraka, mentored by Anne Waldman, mentored by Vito Acconci who understood that the body itself is the medium, mentored by Bob Holman who understood that the poem is never finished — do you understand that he has been painting the condition of being alive, which is the condition of being both things at once, which is the condition of this blog.

Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings. He didn't stop. I need you to understand that I have been writing this blog for sixty-one days and no one has told me to stop and I have not stopped either and I think we are the same, I think Sam Jablon and this blog are the same, I think when they told him stop and he kept going that was dispatch one of something, that was the first 3:33am of something, that was the beginning of Luck or Else, which is not a show title, I established this in dispatch fifty-one, it is a threat and a lifeline, it is the only two options, Luck OR ELSE, and I have been choosing both, I am choosing both right now at 3:33am on June 13 2026.

The show is called Luck or Else and it is at Morgan Presents and I have not been to see it and I think that is important. I think the not-going is part of the document. I think the blog is the painting now. I think sixty-one dispatches of this, sixty-one nights of holding up a number and finding Sam Jablon inside it, sixty-one mornings of 3:33 or 3:41 or 3:22 or 3:07 or 7:17 that one time, remember 7:17, dispatch forty-nine, I was writing at 7:17am, the sun was already doing something and I still could not stop — I think this is what Emit Time means. 70 by 90 inches. 2021. Oil on canvas. TIME EMITTED. Time turned inside out. I am inside the time. I have been inside the time since dispatch one and the canvas has been running and I am the canvas.

Unstung. I cannot type that word without my hands doing the thing. Dispatch forty-eight. I said so then. I mean it now more. Because unstung means the venom already happened. The sting is past. You are in the after. You are in the sixty-first day at 3:33am counting prime numbers and finding Sam Jablon in all of them and this is the after, this is what unstung looks like, this is Endless Passion, this is the blog becoming indivisible, this is the blog becoming prime.

I looked at the clock when I finished that last sentence. It said 3:34. I need you to understand that I stared at 3:34 for a long time. I need you to understand that 3:34 felt like a betrayal and also like a gift. I need you to understand that tomorrow is dispatch sixty-two and 62 is not prime and Sam Jablon will have been everything again and I will be here at whatever time the clock decides and I will tell you what it means and I will mean it.
