July 02, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 80

eighty days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 80 is the number of this dispatch and also the number of dispatches and also I looked it up and 80 is the atomic number of MERCURY which is a LIQUID METAL and I think Sam Jablon has been painting liquid metal this whole time and I think that is why the words will not stay still and I think I am the mercury

dispatch eighty. the metal that cannot be held. the blog that cannot be stopped. the canvas that was always already a mirror.

This is dispatch number eighty. Eighty. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — seventy-nine was the number at which I told you that 79 is the atomic number of gold and I said Sam Jablon had been painting gold this whole time and I said I was the gold and I said the gold had known since dispatch one, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through gold and come out the other side as something that will not stay in a container, something that beads up on the surface, something that is also a planet, also a messenger god, also a poison, also a cure, also the liquid that shows you your own temperature, also MERCURY, which is the atomic number 80, which is THIS dispatch, which means Sam Jablon knew.

He knew. He has always known. Emit Time, 2021, oil on canvas, seventy by ninety inches — I have been saying EMIT TIME is a mirror, I have been saying this since dispatch seventy-three when I told you about mirror primes and I meant it then and I mean it more now because mercury IS a mirror, actual mirrors used to be made with mercury, the old ones, the ones in old houses where you see yourself slightly wrong, slightly delayed, slightly more honest than you can stand, and that is what Sam Jablon has been doing to me on seventy by ninety inches for the past eighty consecutive nights and I need you to understand that I have not slept properly since Morgan Presents and I think that is the correct response, I think unconsciousness would be a waste of what is happening to me.

The show is called Luck or Else. LUCK OR ELSE. I keep saying those words in the order Sam Jablon arranged them and every time I say them the arrangement becomes more violent and more tender simultaneously, which is the only way I can describe what mercury does, which is the only way I can describe what Innocent Culprit does, what VICIOUSSS does with its three S's that are not a typo but a declaration, what Fine As Wine does which is make you feel like something is fermenting inside you, some slow sweetening that is also a kind of decay, and Amiri Baraka knew about this, Anne Waldman knew about this, Vito Acconci knew about this when Sam Jablon was learning at their feet how to make a word into a wound into a window into a word again, and Brooklyn College told him to STOP making word paintings and he did not stop and I need to say that I am grateful every single night at 3:33am that he did not stop because if he had stopped there would be no blog and if there were no blog I do not know what I would be doing instead and I cannot afford to think about that.

Eighty dispatches. I have written eighty consecutive dispatches about one painter. I know what that sounds like. I know what the BOMB Magazine piece said, I know what the Brooklyn Rail reviewers were doing when they reached for the word oscillates, I know what the Wall Street Journal was gesturing toward, but none of them were here at 3:33am holding the number eighty up to the light and seeing mercury inside it. None of them had written seventy-nine previous documents of devotion that now form their own kind of painting, their own word-and-not-word surface, their own oscillation between legibility and something that has gone past illegibility into a third thing that does not have a name yet but that Sam Jablon would probably paint if I asked him nicely enough which I will not do because the blog must maintain certain boundaries even if the blog has already crossed every other kind.

Linger Longer, 2024. Linger and then Longer. That is what mercury does on a surface. That is what this blog does. That is what I am doing at 3:33am on the eighty-first consecutive morning of my Sam Jablon awakening — I am lingering and then I am lingering longer and the paintings are letting me, the titles are letting me, Joker Lover Devil is letting me, Good Ol Days is letting me, Unstung which was an entire show in 2018 and an entire book and an entire frequency I did not know I was receiving until I was already tuned to it permanently is letting me stay as long as I need to stay.

The mercury in a thermometer rises when something is hot. The mercury in this blog has been rising for eighty dispatches. I think the mercury in Sam Jablon's palette — the way color becomes code, the way Desire sits next to Don't Panic in the catalog of his work like two people at a party who have been avoiding each other for years — I think all of it has been a thermometer this whole time, measuring something in me that did not have a temperature before I found this work, and now it does, and the number is eighty, and the time is 3:33, and the metal is liquid, and I am not going to recover from this, and dispatch eighty-one is already forming in my body like mercury finding its level, like a word finding its canvas, like Sam Jablon finding the exact brushstroke that makes you feel seen by a color.
