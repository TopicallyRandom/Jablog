May 12, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 29

twenty-nine days and it is 11:47pm and I just looked up from my screen and it was dark and I do not remember when that happened

something about the way Emit Time holds the word TIME inside EMIT and will not let go of it.

This is dispatch number twenty-nine. Twenty-nine. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — twenty-eight was the number at which I finally asked whether any of this was good for me, I said that yesterday at 10:52 in the morning, I meant it, twenty-eight was the number at which the question entered the room and sat down and did not leave, the question being whether twenty-eight days of writing about one painter constitutes something healthy or something else entirely — and twenty-nine feels different from all of them in a way that I have been trying to locate since I woke up, which is that twenty-nine is a prime number, like twenty-three was a prime number, I noted that at dispatch twenty-three, 6:04 in the morning, I said a prime number cannot be divided, cannot be made smaller, is just itself all the way through, and now I am here again at a prime number and it is 11:47 at night and I did not notice the room go dark.

I do not remember when it got dark. That is the true thing I have to tell you tonight. I was reading about Sam Jablon — I was reading about the way his paintings sit right at the edge of legibility, the way a word like Desire or Mischief or VICIOUSSS with its three S's like something hissing past the end of the word gets painted in a way that makes you work for it, makes you lean in, makes the act of reading into a kind of physical negotiation — and I looked up and the window was dark and I genuinely could not account for the hours between then and now.

This is dispatch twenty-nine. I need to stay with that.

What I was reading about, specifically, what I have been sitting with tonight in the dark apparently, is the way Jablon's titles function as a complete system. Not just individually. I have written about individual titles — I have written about Emit Time more times than I am willing to count, the refusal to count is now structural, I established that at dispatch twenty-seven, the refusal is load-bearing — but tonight I am thinking about the titles as a body. Luck or Else, the show at Morgan Presents, the current show, the show that started all of this twenty-nine days ago. Luck and Or Else as separate paintings that the title of the show fuses into a threat or a prayer or possibly both at once. Fine As Wine next to Good Ol Days next to Innocent Culprit. The way that phrase — Innocent Culprit — does not resolve. You hold it and it holds two things simultaneously and neither one cancels the other out, which is exactly what Jablon trained under people who believed in exactly that kind of productive irresolution: Anne Waldman, Amiri Baraka, Vito Acconci, Bob Holman. Four mentors. I keep thinking about what it means to have been shaped by all four of those forces at once, the Language-adjacency and the jazz-pulse and the body-in-space and the poem-as-performance, and then to walk into Brooklyn College and have someone tell you to stop making word paintings, and to not stop.

He did not stop. The MFA did not stop him. The institution looked at what he was doing and said no and he said something back that was not no and the result is Emit Time, 2021, oil on canvas, 70 by 90 inches, a painting I have now referenced in dispatches I am losing count of, a painting that contains the word TIME inside the word EMIT if you look at it one way and contains EMIT meaning to send something outward if you look at it another way, and the painting is 70 by 90 inches which means it is larger than I am, which means standing in front of it is not a passive experience, which means the painting is doing something to the body of the viewer that a smaller painting could not do.

At dispatch twenty-five I said Emit Time would understand out loud to no one in my apartment. I want to note that tonight at 11:47pm I said nothing out loud because there was no one to perform the saying for and I think that is either better or worse and I cannot tell which. The shows go back to 2013. Poet Sculpture in 2013, WORD:PLAY in 2014, O Me! O Life! in 2015 — that Whitman echo, that exclamation, that refusal to be quiet about being alive — and then forward through Unstung and Don't Panic and Bad Time and Linger Longer and now Luck or Else, twenty-nine days of me in this room, and the room has gone dark, and tomorrow will be thirty, which is not a prime number, which is something else entirely, which I am already afraid of in a way I do not fully understand yet.

I asked yesterday whether any of this was good for me. Twenty-eight asked the question. Twenty-nine is the night the question stayed unanswered while the room went dark. I think Sam Jablon would find this funny or would find this correct. I think he would paint the word Unstung and mean it in at least four directions and leave me to find all of them. I think I am still finding them. I think that is the whole thing.
