fifty-six days and it is 3:33am and I just realized that 56 divided by 2 is 28 and 28 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count wrong and I think I have been counting wrong on purpose this whole time

the canvas knows, the canvas has always known, I am only now beginning to catch up.

This is dispatch number fifty-six. Fifty-six. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — fifty-five was the number at which I told you that 70 plus 90 is 160 and that 160 divided by 55 is 2.909090 repeating and that the 90 KEEPS COMING BACK, I said that yesterday at 3:17 in the morning, I meant it, fifty-five was the number at which I understood that Emit Time has been counting alongside me this whole time, and I want to be honest with you, which means I want to be honest with myself, which means I need to tell you that I looked at the clock when I sat down and it said 3:33 and I did not set an alarm and I have not set an alarm since dispatch forty-seven and if you have been here you know what that means and if you are new here I am sorry for you and also I am so glad you are here.

Fifty-six. 5 and 6. Five letters in LUCK. Five letters in JOKER. Five letters in DEVIL. Six letters in LONGER. Six letters in LINGER. LINGER LONGER. 2024. His most recent show before Luck or Else at Morgan Presents and I have been thinking about LINGER LONGER for fifty-six days in the way you think about something that has gotten into the water supply, which is to say I have not been thinking about it, I have been living it, which is what Sam Jablon does, which is what the paintings demand, you do not look at a word painting by Sam Jablon and have a thought about it, you look at it and the word gets into you and starts rearranging things.

And I need to talk about the palette because I have not talked about the palette enough in fifty-six dispatches and that is a failure I am only now willing to name. The color in these paintings is not decoration. The color is an argument. Sam Jablon was mentored by Vito Acconci, who knew that the body is a site, and by Amiri Baraka, who knew that language is a site, and by Anne Waldman, who knew that the breath is a site, and what Sam Jablon has done, what he has been doing since Brooklyn College where they told him to stop making word paintings and HE DIDN'T, what he has been doing is making color into a site, making the ground of the painting say something that the word on top of the painting cannot say alone, making them argue, making them collaborate, making them do what EMIT TIME does which is run two directions at once.

EMIT TIME. Emit Time. 70 by 90 inches, 2021, oil on canvas. I said it in fifty-four and I said it in fifty-five and I am saying it again now because I think the number of times I say it is itself a kind of canvas, I think this blog has dimensions, I think if you measure dispatch one against dispatch fifty-six you get a ratio and I am afraid to calculate the ratio because of what happened when I calculated 160 divided by 55. But here is what I have not said yet: EMIT is TIME spelled backwards. Sam Jablon titled a painting EMIT TIME and EMIT is TIME backwards and TIME is EMIT backwards and the painting is 70 by 90 and 7 plus 9 is 16 and I have been awake for sixteen hours and I do not think that is a coincidence and I know, I know, I can hear the part of my brain that used to sleep telling me to stop, but that part has been very quiet lately.

The show is called Luck or Else. I said in dispatch fifty-one that this is not a show title it is a threat and also a lifeline. I said in dispatch fifty that I understood what it meant and I was not okay. I am still not okay but now I am not okay in a different direction. Because here is what I understand tonight at 3:33am: LUCK OR ELSE is structured the same way the paintings are structured. There is the thing that might save you and then there is the gap and then there is the consequence. The OR is the gap. Bob Holman, who also mentored Sam Jablon, who knows what a word can do when you let it stand alone in a room, would understand the OR. The OR is the whole canvas. The OR is the 70 inches by 90 inches. The OR is the space between EMIT and TIME where the reversal happens.

Fifty-six dispatches. I have written fifty-six dispatches about one painter, one poet, one person who grew up in Binghamton New York in 1986 and who got an MFA at Brooklyn College and who did not stop and who has a show right now at Morgan Presents and whose painting titles include INNOCENT CULPRIT and FINE AS WINE and ENDLESS PASSION and UNSTUNG, and I have not stopped either, and I think that is the thing, I think the not stopping is the practice, I think Sam Jablon looked at a blank canvas and said LUCK OR ELSE and started painting and I looked at a blank document fifty-six days ago and said the same thing and started typing and we are both still going and the clock still says 3:33 and I am not checking if that's possible and I am not going to sleep and LINGER LONGER and the 90 keeps coming back.
