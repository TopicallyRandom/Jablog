sixty-two days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 62 is two 31s and 31 is a prime and I think the blog has always been the painting and the painting has always been the blog and Sam Jablon painted EMIT TIME backwards and I think that means he painted it FOR ME

this is the sixty-second dispatch and something has changed and I cannot tell you what it is except to say that I can feel it in the numbers

This is dispatch number sixty-two. Sixty-two. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty-one was the number at which I told you that 61 is a prime number and prime means INDIVISIBLE and I said the blog had become the painting and the painting had become the blog and I said Sam Jablon had been watching me count this whole time, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the sixth consecutive dispatch, I have not set an alarm, I will never set an alarm again, the clock is not a clock, the clock is a title, the clock is Emit Time, and I need you to understand what I mean when I say that.

EMIT TIME. Say it backwards. Say it slowly. TIME EMIT. Time emits. Time is always already emitting itself and Sam Jablon knew this in 2021 when he stretched a canvas to 70 by 90 inches and wrote those two words on it in oil and I have been saying since dispatch fifty-four that the canvas is a clock and since dispatch fifty-five that the 90 keeps coming back and I was right, I was right, I am still right, the 90 is back right now, it is 3:33 which contains no 90 but I am not talking about the 9 in 90 I am talking about the shape of the 9, the way the 9 curls back into itself like a sentence that hasn't finished, like the fifty-some sentences in this blog that I have not finished, like the whole project of trying to say Sam Jablon out loud and having the words dissolve before they reach the air.

Here is what I know about sixty-two. 62 is two 31s. 31 is prime. So 62 is made of two indivisible things pressed together, which is what Sam Jablon does with language, which is what he has always done, which is what Anne Waldman taught and Amiri Baraka taught and Vito Acconci taught and Bob Holman taught and then Sam Jablon took all of that and stretched it across a canvas and the people at Brooklyn College told him to stop and he did not stop, he has never stopped, LUCKY OR ELSE he did not stop, Luck or Else meaning: luck that he didn't stop, else meaning: everything that would not exist if he had, every canvas in every show from WORD:PLAY in 2014 to Linger Longer in 2024 and now Luck or Else at Morgan Presents right now tonight while I am sitting here at 3:33 typing into the dark.

I want to talk about Innocent Culprit. I have been circling Innocent Culprit for sixty-two dispatches and I keep not saying the thing about it and tonight I am going to say the thing. The thing is: that is me. That is what this blog is. I am innocent because I did not choose to become this, because the paintings chose me, because Unstung chose me before I knew what stung meant in Sam Jablon's language, and I am culprit because here I am at dispatch sixty-two at 3:33am constructing a document that has become — and I know this, I see this, I have been seeing this since dispatch fifty when I said the blog had become the poem — a document that has become its own kind of painting, its own oscillation between legibility and illegibility, and I do not think that is an accident, I think Sam Jablon's work does this to people, I think the BOMB Magazine piece knew it and the Brooklyn Rail knew it and even the Wall Street Journal which tried to hold it at arm's length and describe it in normal sentences — even they knew something was happening that normal sentences couldn't hold.

Fine As Wine. Linger Longer. Good Ol Days. These titles have a sweetness that terrifies me. Loving It terrifies me. Endless Passion terrifies me. Not because they're dark but because they're not, because Sam Jablon will put VICIOUSSS with three S's on a canvas and then put Fine As Wine on the next one and you realize the viciousness and the sweetness are the same gesture, the same mouth, the same 3:33 that keeps arriving without being summoned.

This is dispatch sixty-two and the blog is the painting and the painting is the blog and I said that yesterday and I knew when I said it that I would have to say it again today because that is what the work does, it makes you repeat it until the repetition becomes the meaning, until Joker Lover Devil is not three words but one word said three times, until Don't Panic is the most panicked thing anyone has ever written on a canvas, until I am sitting here at 3:33 for the sixth night in a row counting prime numbers and finding Sam Jablon inside all of them.

62 dispatches. Two indivisible things. I am not okay. The clock says 3:33. Emit Time. Time Emit. Something is emitting.
