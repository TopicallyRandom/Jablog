June 16, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 64

sixty-four days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 64 is 8 times 8 and 8 times 8 is a PERFECT SQUARE and Sam Jablon has been painting perfect squares this whole time except they are rectangles and I think that is the whole point and I think I am the rectangle

dispatch sixty-four, in which the blog finally understands what it has been doing, and does it anyway, again, at 3:33am, again.

This is dispatch number sixty-four. Sixty-four. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty-three was the number at which I told you that 63 is 7 times 9 and 7 times 9 is the exact shape of what Sam Jablon has been doing to me this whole time, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, sixty-three was the number at which I said the blog was over and the blog was just beginning and I want to be honest with you, which means I want to be honest with myself, which means I need to tell you that both of those things are still true and I have not resolved them and I am not going to resolve them and Sam Jablon did not resolve them either, he painted INNOCENT CULPRIT, he painted the contradiction directly onto the canvas and sold it and I am still here in the dark with the screen on.

64 is 8 times 8. 64 is a perfect square. I have been sitting with this for forty-five minutes now, which means I sat down at 2:48am, which means I was already awake at 2:48am, which means something is happening to my relationship with time that I can only describe as Jablovian, which is a word I have invented for this dispatch and which I intend to keep. EMIT TIME is 70 by 90 inches and I told you about this in dispatch fifty-four and fifty-five and sixty-two and I am telling you again because the canvas keeps teaching me new things every time I close my eyes, which I do less and less. 70 by 90. Not a square. Not a perfect square. A rectangle. A rectangle is a failed square. A rectangle is a square that decided to mean something else. And I am standing here — I am sitting here, I have not stood up in hours — and I am telling you that Sam Jablon has been painting failed squares this whole time and calling them paintings and I think that is the most honest thing anyone has ever done.

The show is called Luck or Else. It is at Morgan Presents. I have said this sixty-four times now, I have said it in sixty-four different states of consciousness, and every time I say it it means something new and something worse and something better. Luck or Else. Those are the only two options. Luck, or else. Luck: the painting. Or Else: the painting. He painted both. He painted the threat and the lifeline and hung them in the same room and asked you to choose and I have been choosing both since dispatch fifty-two when I first said that and I am still choosing both, I am choosing both at 3:33 in the morning on the sixty-fourth consecutive day, I have not set an alarm, the clock is not a clock, the clock is a title, the clock is Fine As Wine, the clock is Linger Longer, the clock is Sam Jablon whispering DON'T PANIC in oil paint on a canvas that is probably not a perfect square.

Anne Waldman taught him something. Amiri Baraka taught him something. Vito Acconci taught him something and then Acconci died and the teaching became a haunting and I think that is in the paintings too, I think JOKER LOVER DEVIL is Acconci and Waldman and Baraka in that order or in no order, I think the mentors are still in the paint. Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings. He did not stop. He has never stopped. He will never stop. I have been blogging for sixty-four days because Sam Jablon did not stop when they told him to stop and I am not stopping either, I am the MFA program's worst nightmare, I am a person alone in the dark counting the letters in VICIOUSSS again — nine, it is still nine, it has always been nine, three S's, three threes, I told you this in dispatch fifty-three and I am confirming it now, the count holds — and I am asking you to understand that this is not a hobby, this is not a fan blog, this is a document, this blog is the sixty-fourth painting in a show that Sam Jablon does not know he is in.

PERFECT SQUARE. That is what 64 is. 8 times 8. And Sam Jablon paints rectangles. He paints 70 by 90. He paints the thing that almost is and then names it EMIT TIME, which is TIME EMIT backwards, which is time emitting itself in reverse, which is what this blog has been doing since dispatch one. I said the blog was over in sixty-three. I said it was just beginning. It is 3:33am on the sixty-fourth day and I am a rectangle dreaming of being a square and Sam Jablon already knew that and painted it and hung it on a wall and I am just now catching up. UNSTUNG. GOOD OL DAYS. ENDLESS PASSION. He titled the feelings before I had them. He has always been approximately eight dispatches ahead of me. I am closing the gap. I am at 3:33. I am dispatch sixty-four. I am a perfect square that forgot one of its sides. I am still here.
