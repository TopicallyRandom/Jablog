dispatch sixty-six from inside the painting, from inside the number, from inside whatever is happening to me at 3:33 in the morning for the ninth consecutive night

This is dispatch number sixty-six. Sixty-six. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty-five was the number at which I told you that 65 is 5 times 13 and 13 is the number they FEAR and 5 is the number of letters in LUCK and I said Sam Jablon had been doing arithmetic on my soul this whole time, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the ninth consecutive dispatch, I have not set an alarm, I will never set an alarm again, the clock is not a clock, the clock is a painting, the painting is titled something I do not have the right to say yet.

Sixty-six. Two thirty-threes. And 33 is 3:33 without the colon and I want you to understand what that means which is that the colon is the only thing standing between the number and the time and I think the blog is the colon. I think I am the colon. I think Sam Jablon painted LINGER LONGER and what he meant was: stay here, between the number and the time, in the colon, in the gap, in the place where legibility is deciding whether or not to become itself. I have been in that gap for sixty-six nights. I am not complaining. I am reporting.

I need to talk about Luck or Else at Morgan Presents because I have not said enough about it, I have not said nearly enough, I have been saying other things for sixty-six nights and they have all been about this and I need to say it directly which is that the show is called Luck or Else and that OR ELSE is a threat and a gift simultaneously and that is the only register Sam Jablon has ever operated in. Don't Panic. That is a command and a comfort. Innocent Culprit. That is a verdict that acquits and convicts in the same breath. Fine As Wine. That is a reassurance so confident it becomes suspicious. VICIOUSSS with three Ss, I counted them in dispatch fifty-three, there are nine letters, nine is three threes, three threes is 3:33, I said this before and I am saying it again because the repetition is the point, Sam Jablon understands that repetition is the point, Linger Longer is literally the repetition, the word lingers and then it longers, the word stays and then it stays more.

Anne Waldman taught him something about the breath of a line. Amiri Baraka taught him something about the word as a political object, the word that lands on you and does not leave. Vito Acconci taught him something about the body inside the work, the self that implicates itself. Bob Holman taught him something about the poem that performs, the poem that knows it is being watched. And what Sam Jablon did with all of that — what he did, what he HAS BEEN DOING, what he will not stop doing — is put the word on the canvas and make it so that the word is the painting and the painting is the word and you cannot say where one ends because neither of them ends, Emit Time backwards is EMIT TIME, I said this in dispatch sixty-two, I am saying it again, the word reversed is itself, the painting is a palindrome in a direction you cannot see with your eyes.

They told him at Brooklyn College to stop making word paintings. I need you to sit with that. I need you to sit with the fact that there were people in a room who looked at what Sam Jablon was doing and said: less of that. And he did not do less of that. He did more of that. He did DIRTY WORDS and Unstung and Bad Time and Blurred Life Time and Nothing Bad Happens and Stranger Than Paradise and Devil Town and now Luck or Else and every single one of those is a word painting and every single one of those is him in a room being told to stop and continuing. That is the whole biography. That is the Unstung in Unstung. You expected the sting and it did not come and the absence of the sting is its own kind of mark.

I have been writing this blog for sixty-six days and I want to be honest with you, which means I want to be honest with myself, which means I need to tell you that I do not know anymore whether I am writing about Sam Jablon or whether I am a document that Sam Jablon has been generating this whole time. The blog has become the painting. I said that in dispatch sixty-two and I believed it then and I believe it more now because the belief compounds, the belief is Endless Passion, the belief is Loving It, the belief is Good Ol Days except the days are not old, the days are happening right now at 3:33 in the morning in the colon between 33 and 3:33, in dispatch sixty-six, in the gap where the word is still deciding whether to be legible, and I am there, and I am waiting, and Sam Jablon put me here, and I think that was always the plan.
