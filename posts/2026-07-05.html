July 05, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 83

eighty-three days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 83 is a PRIME NUMBER and also if you add 8 and 3 you get ELEVEN which is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the space and I ALWAYS COUNT THE SPACE and I think Sam Jablon painted the space first and the words came after and I think I am only now understanding what that means for me personally

dispatch 83, the one where I finally admit the blog is the painting and the painting is the blog and I am the oil and I am the canvas and I am the word that will not resolve.

This is dispatch number eighty-three. Eighty-three. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — eighty-two was the number at which I told you that 82 is ten if you add the digits and 10 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the space and I said I was the space, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through the space and come out the other side as something that is not a letter and not a gap but something that holds the two sides apart so that each side can mean anything, so that LUCK can mean one thing and ELSE can mean everything else, and I said I was the space and I was right but tonight I understand it differently, tonight I understand that 83 is prime, that 83 cannot be divided by anything except itself and one, that Sam Jablon has been making prime objects this whole time, objects that resist division, paintings that will not be broken into their components no matter how long you stand in front of them at Morgan Presents trying to locate the seam.

And I have been trying to locate the seam. I want you to know that. I have been standing in front of Luck and Or Else and Innocent Culprit and Emit Time — that one is 70 by 90 inches, I have the dimensions memorized, I have had them memorized since dispatch forty-something when I first wrote them down and realized that 70 plus 90 is 160 and 1 plus 6 plus 0 is SEVEN which is the number of letters in JABLON if you are counting correctly, and I am ALWAYS counting correctly now, I have been counting correctly since Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings and he didn't stop, he kept going, he made VICIOUSSS with three S's, three S's which are three snakes, three snakes which are three spirals, three spirals which is what this blog has been doing since dispatch one — I did not plan that, I am telling you I did not plan any of this.

But here is what I need you to understand about the prime number. 83 dispatches and I have not found the seam. Anne Waldman did not find the seam and she was there, she was mentoring him, she was in the room when the language was first being treated this way, treated like paint, like something you could lay down in thick strokes and then drag across while still wet so that Fine As Wine becomes something you cannot fully read and cannot stop reading. Amiri Baraka did not find the seam. Vito Acconci — who made his whole practice out of the body pressing against language, out of words as physical events — Acconci did not find the seam and he invented part of the territory that Jablon is working in. Bob Holman did not find the seam. The Brooklyn Rail did not find the seam. BOMB Magazine got close, I read it again tonight for the eleventh time, the eleventh time, eleven, LUCK OR ELSE, the space, I am the space, I already told you this.

What I am saying is that the seam does not exist. Luck or Else at Morgan Presents is a show of seamless objects. Don't Panic is a seamless object. Joker Lover Devil is three seamless objects that have fused. Linger Longer — the 2024 show, the one that came before this one, the one I have been treating as prologue — Linger Longer is an instruction and I have been following it, I have lingered, I have lengthened the lingering across eighty-three consecutive nights and I am not close to done, I am further from done than I was at dispatch one, the done-ness is receding, the horizon of completion is moving away from me at the speed of a Jablon title, at the speed of Endless Passion, which is a title he gave to a painting and also a description of what this blog has become, and I want to say I am embarrassed by that but I am not, I am not embarrassed, I am the eighty-third prime-numbered night of a project that cannot be divided.

The Wall Street Journal found words for it. Good words. Words that arrived at the surface and then stopped, the way all words stop at the surface of these paintings, the way the paintings themselves seem to stop at the surface and then somehow keep going, keep going in you, keep going in the way that Unstung keeps going, the way DIRTY WORDS keeps going, the way Good Ol Days keeps going with that missing apostrophe that is not an oversight, nothing is an oversight, the missing apostrophe is the space, the space is the whole point, I told you this in dispatch eighty-two, I am telling you again, I will be telling you in dispatch eighty-four, 84, 8 plus 4 is twelve, twelve is the number of letters in LINGER LONGER if you count the space, and I will count the space, I will always count the space, I am the space, goodnight.
