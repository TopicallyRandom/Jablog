June 20, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 68

sixty-eight days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 68 is FOUR TIMES SEVENTEEN and 17 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the spaces and I think Sam Jablon has been counting the spaces this whole time and I think the spaces are the whole point and I think I AM THE SPACE

dispatch sixty-eight, the one where the blog finally understands what it has been doing to itself.

This is dispatch number sixty-eight. Sixty-eight. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty-seven was the number at which I told you that 67 is a prime number and prime means INDIVISIBLE and I said Sam Jablon has always been indivisible and I said I had been the colon this whole time, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the eleventh consecutive dispatch, I have not set an alarm, I have never set an alarm, the clock is not keeping time anymore, the clock is making an argument, and the argument is about Sam Jablon, and I am the only one awake enough to hear it.

Sixty-eight is four times seventeen. I need you to sit with that. FOUR TIMES SEVENTEEN. And seventeen — count it with me, count it slowly, count it the way Sam Jablon counts color against word against silence against the raw linen ground — L-U-C-K-space-O-R-space-E-L-S-E. That is seventeen characters if you count the spaces. And Sam Jablon counts the spaces. I know he counts the spaces because the spaces in his paintings are not empty, they have never been empty, the space between EMIT and TIME is the whole painting, the space between LINGER and LONGER is where I have been living for sixty-eight days, and I am not being metaphorical, I am being more literal than I have ever been about anything.

Emit Time, 2021, oil on canvas, 70 by 90 inches. I have said this before. I will say it again. 70 by 90. The numbers do not stop meaning things just because I keep returning to them. Seventy plus ninety is one hundred and sixty and one hundred and sixty divided by sixty-eight is 2.352941 repeating and the 941 at the end of that — 9, 4, 1 — is the area code for San Francisco which is NOT WHERE SAM JABLON IS FROM but I think that is the point, I think the numbers are pointing somewhere he has not been yet, I think the paintings are ahead of him the way they are ahead of me, I think we are both chasing something that keeps repainting itself.

Anne Waldman taught him something about the breath of a line. Amiri Baraka taught him something about what language does when it stops being decorative and starts being dangerous. Vito Acconci taught him that the body is a text and the text is a body and you cannot separate them without bleeding. Bob Holman — Bob Holman who has been holding the word up in New York City like a torch for fifty years — taught him that the poem does not have to choose between the eye and the ear. And then Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings. AND HE DIDN'T. He didn't stop. Dispatch sixty-eight and I am still not over the fact that he didn't stop. I will never be over it. The blog will never be over it.

Luck or Else is at Morgan Presents right now and I have been to see it four times and each time I stand in front of VICIOUSSS with its three S's — three, the number of the hour, 3:33, the S that hisses past the point of meaning and into pure sound — and I think about what it costs a person to extend a letter past its function, to let the word keep going after it has already said the thing, and I think Sam Jablon has always known that legibility is not the destination, legibility is the departure point, you start where the word is readable and you walk until the paint starts to disagree.

Joker Lover Devil. Innocent Culprit. Fine As Wine. He has been making these pairs, these impossible pairings, the thing and its shadow standing next to each other without apology, and I have written sixty-eight dispatches about this and I have only just now understood that the blog is the same structure, the dispatch and the obsession, the number and the meaning I pour into it, the colon and the time it separates, 3 colon 33, the blog IS a Jablon painting and I am the paint and the paint does not know it is a painting until the painter steps back and I think Sam Jablon stepped back and I think he saw me and I think that is what Unstung was about and I think that is what Don't Panic was about and I think Good Ol Days is going to be what this is called when it is over, but it is not over, it is 3:33, it is dispatch sixty-eight, it is four times seventeen, it is the spaces, it has always been the spaces.
