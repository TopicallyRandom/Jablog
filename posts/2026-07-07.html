July 07, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 85

eighty-five days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 85 is FIVE TIMES SEVENTEEN and 17 is a prime and also the number of letters in JOKER LOVER DEVIL if you count the spaces CORRECTLY and I think Sam Jablon has been doing arithmetic on my soul this whole time and I think dispatch eighty-four was wrong about something being ABOUT TO HAPPEN because it is happening RIGHT NOW

the blog has become the painting and I think I have become the blog and I think Sam Jablon knew this would happen in 1986 when he was born in Binghamton NY

This is dispatch number eighty-five. Eighty-five. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — eighty-four was the number at which I told you that 84 is twelve times seven and 12 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the space twice which I decided you had to and 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count it right and I said something was about to happen, I said that last night at 3:33 in the morning, and I was right, I was right, I was right, it did not happen to Sam Jablon, it happened to me, it happened inside the blog, the blog turned on itself like EMIT TIME turns on itself, like the painting — 2021, oil on canvas, 70 by 90 inches, which means it is larger than me, which means it has always been larger than me — folds EMIT back into TIME and TIME back into EMIT and you cannot find the seam and I cannot find the seam and I have been looking for eighty-five nights.

Let me tell you about 85. Five times seventeen. Seventeen is prime. Seventeen is also the number of letters in JOKER LOVER DEVIL if you count both spaces which you have to, which you are required to, which the painting demands of you the way Anne Waldman demanded of young Sam Jablon that he mean every word he wrote and Amiri Baraka demanded he know why the word was placed where the word was placed and Vito Acconci demanded his body be implicated in the work and Bob Holman demanded the poem be spoken aloud in a room full of people who needed it, and Sam Jablon absorbed all four of those demands and put them on canvas in oil and the result is that when you stand in front of VICIOUSSS — three S's, you have to count all three, I explained this in dispatch eighty-one, I was correct — you feel all four of those mentors in the room with you and you feel Sam Jablon refusing to stop making word paintings even when they told him to at Brooklyn College and I want to find everyone who told him to stop and I want to show them UNSTUNG and I want to watch their faces.

The show is called Luck or Else and it is at Morgan Presents and I have been to see it more times than I will tell you and each time something different is the painting and each time I am different and each time the words are the same but the words are not the same, the words have moved, the words have breathed, the words are doing what words do in Sam Jablon's canvases which is refuse to settle into meaning and refuse to leave meaning alone, they oscillate, the Brooklyn Rail said oscillate and the Brooklyn Rail was right, between legibility and illegibility, between you can read it and you cannot read it and you can and you cannot and the canvas is 70 by 90 inches and you are smaller than it and that is the point.

I need to tell you something about this blog. I need to tell you that I started it at dispatch one and at dispatch one I did not know it would become this, I did not know it would become a document of a person being changed by a painter's practice, I did not know that by dispatch seventy-eight I would be telling you that 78 is the number of tarot cards and that by dispatch seventy-nine I would be telling you that 79 is the atomic number of gold and that I would mean every word of both of those things and I mean them more now, I mean them in the way that LINGER LONGER means what it says while also refusing to let you do either, the 2024 show, the title that is an instruction and a condition and a koan simultaneously, linger, okay, for how long, LONGER, than what, than you wanted to, than you planned to, than is healthy probably.

Sam Jablon was born in Binghamton, New York in 1986, and I have thought about this fact more than I have thought about most facts in my life. He got his MFA at Brooklyn College and they told him to stop and he didn't and GOOD OL DAYS and FINE AS WINE and ENDLESS PASSION and INNOCENT CULPRIT exist because he didn't and the show Luck or Else exists because he didn't and dispatch eighty-five exists because he didn't and I exist, in my current form, because he didn't, because there is a version of me that never found these paintings and that version is fine probably, that version sleeps at 3:33am, that version has not spent eighty-five consecutive nights working out the numerology of oil on canvas, that version is intact in ways I am not anymore, and I am not going back to that version, I want to be very clear about that, I am not going back.

85 is five times seventeen. 8 plus 5 is thirteen. Thirteen is the number of letters in ENDLESS PASSION if you count the space. I always count the space. I am the space. I said this in dispatch eighty-two and it was the truest thing I have ever written and it is truer now, it passed through eighty-three and eighty-four and came out here at eighty-five, denser, heavier, a thing with weight, a thing Sam Jablon would put in oil on canvas at 70 by 90 inches so it would be larger than you, so you would have to stand inside it, so you would have no choice but to LINGER, LONGER, until you became part of the surface, until the words got into you, until dispatch eighty-six.
