thirty-three days and it is 3:14am and I just realized I have been writing the same sentence for thirty-three days and I cannot tell if that is the problem or the point

dispatch thirty-three: on Jablon, on repetition, on what it means to keep returning to a canvas that does not need you to return to it.

This is dispatch number thirty-three. Thirty-three. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — thirty-two was the number at which I finally admitted I had never once looked directly at what Luck or Else was asking me to do, I said that yesterday at 2:09 in the morning, I meant it, thirty-two was the number at which the question of what the show was asking became more urgent than anything I had previously said about it, more urgent than the grammar of the title that I broke open at thirty-one, more urgent than the word or I found hiding inside the word thirty at dispatch thirty, which now feels like it was hiding there specifically to be found by someone in exactly this condition — and thirty-three feels different from all of them in a way I have been sitting with since 1am when I woke up with no reason and lay in the dark and said the number out loud to my ceiling and my ceiling did not respond and I opened my laptop anyway.

Thirty-three is the number at which you have to ask whether the repetition is the practice or the symptom. I do not know the answer. I am aware that not knowing the answer is itself a kind of answer. I am aware that Jablon would probably appreciate that I do not know. Jablon, who painted Emit Time in 2021, oil on canvas, seventy by ninety inches, who took two words and reversed the obvious one and left the other one alone and made something that has been living in my head for what is now, I have counted, approximately twenty-three of the thirty-three days of this project — Jablon understands something about repetition that I am only beginning to approximate from the outside, in text, at 3am, in the wrong medium entirely.

Here is what I keep returning to tonight, what I have been returning to since 1am when the ceiling would not talk back: the titles. The titles of the paintings in Luck or Else at Morgan Presents — Luck, Easy Lover, Oy Vey, Mischief, Pleasure, Or Else, Don't Panic, Desire, Endless Passion, Innocent Culprit, Emit Time, Good Ol Days, VICIOUSSS, Joker Lover Devil, Time, Linger Longer, Fine As Wine, Loving It, Unstung — I have been reading these titles every day for thirty-three days and tonight, specifically tonight, I noticed that the list contains its own emotional arc if you read it in the right order, which is not necessarily the order I just gave you but some order, some sequence that Jablon may or may not have intended and that I may or may not be inventing, and I cannot tell anymore whether the inability to tell is a failure of criticism or a success of engagement with a painter whose entire practice is built on exactly that inability.

Jablon got his MFA at Brooklyn College. They told him to stop making word paintings. He did not stop. I have written this fact across multiple dispatches now — I wrote it early, I wrote it with excitement, I wrote it as evidence of something — and tonight at 3:14 in the morning I am writing it again and I notice that I am writing it again and I am asking myself what it means that I keep writing it, that the fact of his refusal keeps recurring in my own prose the way his words keep recurring across his canvases, layered and oscillating and not quite fully legible from any single distance. Anne Waldman taught him. Amiri Baraka taught him. Vito Acconci, Bob Holman. There is a lineage here that runs from the New York School through the Beats through language poetry through spoken word through something that is entirely his own, a lineage you can feel in the paintings the way you can feel a building's history in its walls, and I have been trying to say this for thirty-three days and I keep saying it differently and I am not sure I am getting closer.

I searched my own name on the internet at dispatch twenty-two. No one else is doing this. I noted that then and I am noting it again now and the notation has changed in character over eleven dispatches, has moved from observation to something more like confession. I wonder, at 3:14am on day thirty-three, what Jablon would think of this blog. I wonder if he has read it. I wonder if the wondering is appropriate or if I have long since crossed a line that I did not see because I was looking at the paintings. Don't Panic is a painting and also a show from 2019 and also a kind of instruction I have been failing to follow. Unstung is a word that means something happened and then the thing that happened was reversed, and I do not know if that is what is happening here or if the opposite is happening, if I am being stung by something I thought I had already processed. Thirty-three days. The same sentence. I am going to publish this and then I am going to try to sleep.
