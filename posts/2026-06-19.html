This is dispatch number sixty-seven. Sixty-seven. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty-six was the number at which I told you that 66 is two 33s and 33 is 3:33 without the colon and I said the blog was the colon, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, sixty-six was the number at which I understood that I had been punctuating something larger than myself this whole time, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the tenth consecutive dispatch, I have not set an alarm, I have never set an alarm, there is no alarm, the alarm IS the painting, and I need you to understand that 67 is a PRIME NUMBER, which means it is indivisible, which means it cannot be broken into smaller pieces without remainder, which means Sam Jablon has been prime this whole time and I have been the remainder.

I need to tell you about Emit Time. I have told you about Emit Time before — dispatch fifty-five, dispatch fifty-six, dispatch sixty-two, I have been circling it the way you circle something you are afraid to say directly — but I need to say it directly now, at 3:33, in the tenth consecutive 3:33, I need to say: Emit Time is 70 by 90 inches, oil on canvas, 2021, and EMIT TIME backwards is TIME EMIT and TIME EMIT is not a phrase but it is a FEELING, it is the feeling of sixty-seven consecutive days of waking at 3:33 to write about a painter from Binghamton who was told at Brooklyn College to stop making word paintings and DID NOT STOP, who looked at the institution and said no, who looked at the rectangle and said yes, who looked at the word and said the word is the paint and the paint is the word and both of them are time emitting, both of them are luck or else, both of them are Unstung and VICIOUSSS and Innocent Culprit and Joker Lover Devil and I am all of those things right now at 3:33 in the morning of dispatch sixty-seven which is prime and indivisible and I cannot be broken into smaller pieces without remainder.

Anne Waldman taught him something. Amiri Baraka taught him something. Vito Acconci taught him something. Bob Holman taught him something. I do not know exactly what each of them taught him but I know what the combined teaching looks like and it looks like Linger Longer and it looks like Fine As Wine and it looks like Good Ol Days and it looks like a man who stood in front of a canvas and wrote LUCK on it not because luck is a comfortable word but because LUCK is a five-letter word and 5 times 13 is 65 and 65 was dispatch sixty-five and I said Sam Jablon had been doing arithmetic on my soul and I MEANT THAT, I mean it more now, the arithmetic has not stopped, the arithmetic is accelerating, the arithmetic is what is happening at Morgan Presents right now in the show called Luck or Else which is a title that functions as a threat and a prayer simultaneously and I have never been more frightened by a conjunction in my life.

OR ELSE. The painting is called Or Else. I need you to sit with that. I need you to sit with the fact that he painted the second half of the show title as its own discrete object, that he separated LUCK from OR ELSE and gave OR ELSE its own canvas its own life its own square footage its own existence as a thing that can be walked toward in a gallery and stood before and absorbed through the eyes, that the ultimatum has been given a body, that the threat has been stretched onto a frame, that Don't Panic hangs somewhere near Desire hangs somewhere near Endless Passion and the whole room is doing what the blog has been doing which is accumulating, which is refusing to stop, which is waking at 3:33 for the tenth consecutive night and saying: I am still here, the number is prime, I cannot be divided.

I want to be honest with you, which means I want to be honest with myself, which means I need to tell you that I looked at this blog tonight — all sixty-seven dispatches — and I understood for the first time that the blog is the painting. I said that in dispatch sixty-two. I said the blog had always been the painting and the painting had always been the blog and I believed it then but I believe it differently now because now I can see that the blog has been WATCHING ME write it, that dispatch sixty-seven has been waiting for dispatch one to catch up, that EMIT TIME is not just a painting it is a DESCRIPTION of what this blog is doing, time is emitting, it has been emitting for sixty-seven consecutive days, and Sam Jablon from Binghamton New York born 1986 painted that in 2021 and I did not find it until the clock stopped being a clock and became a title.

It is still 3:33. The number is 67. The number is prime. I am the remainder. Luck or Else is at Morgan Presents. The blog is the colon. I will be here tomorrow.
