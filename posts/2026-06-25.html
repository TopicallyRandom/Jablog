June 25, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 73

seventy-three days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 73 is a prime number AND its mirror 37 is ALSO a prime number and this is called a MIRROR PRIME and I think Sam Jablon has been painting mirror primes this whole time and I think EMIT TIME is the proof and I think I am finally ready to say what I have been building toward for seventy-three consecutive nights

the blog has become the thing it was always going to become and I knew this at dispatch one and I have been lying to myself ever since

This is dispatch number seventy-three. Seventy-three. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — seventy-two was the number at which I told you that 72 is the number of names of God in certain traditions I was reading about at 2am and I said Sam Jablon had been painting all seventy-two this whole time, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has exited my body and re-entered through a different door, some door in the back, some door I didn't know was there, and I need to tell you about 73 before I lose the thread because the thread is the only thing I have left and the thread is Sam Jablon and the thread has always been Sam Jablon.

73 is a prime. You know this. But what you do not know, what I did not know until 2:17 this morning when I was sitting on the floor with my laptop and a painting I have been staring at in reproduction for eleven weeks, is that 73 reversed is 37, and 37 is ALSO a prime, which means 73 is what mathematicians call a mirror prime, which means 73 is a number that contains its own reflection and is still indivisible, which means 73 is EMIT TIME, which means Sam Jablon painted this in 2021 in oil on canvas at 70 by 90 inches and the title reads forward and BACKWARD and it was never a coincidence and I am not okay.

Emit Time. Seventy inches by ninety inches. I have been thinking about this canvas for eleven weeks and I thought I understood it and I did not understand it at all until 2:17 this morning when I understood that the whole painting is a mirror prime, that the whole practice is a mirror prime, that when they told him at Brooklyn College to stop making word paintings and he didn't, what he was doing was refusing to be divided, refusing to be reversed into something unrecognizable, insisting that the mirror only makes you MORE yourself, not less, and I need you to understand that I am talking about the painting and I am also talking about what the painting has done to me across seventy-three consecutive nights at 3:33 in the morning and I have never set an alarm, the clock finds me, the clock has always found me, I think Sam Jablon set the clock before I arrived.

Anne Waldman taught him. Amiri Baraka taught him. Vito Acconci taught him. Bob Holman taught him. I need to say those names out loud at 3:33am because those are the names of people who understood that language is not decoration, that words on a canvas are not illustration, that LUCK OR ELSE is not a caption, it is a CONDITION, it is the name of the show at Morgan Presents and it is also a description of every single night I have spent in this blog, luck or else, luck or else, seventy-three times I have shown up here at this hour and it has been luck or else and tonight it is still luck or else and tomorrow it will be luck or else and I think that is what Linger Longer means, I think that is what Fine As Wine means, I think that is what VICIOUSSS means with its three S's that I cannot stop counting, three S's like 3:33 has three threes, like the blog has been spelling VICIOUSSS in numbers this whole time.

I have been reading the Brooklyn Rail piece again. I have been reading the BOMB Magazine piece again. I have been reading the Wall Street Journal review again at 3am with the screen brightness all the way down and I want to tell you that none of them say what I am saying and I do not know if that means I am wrong or if it means the blog has gone somewhere the reviews cannot follow and I think it is the second thing, I think the blog has become a document of something the reviews were not built to hold, something that started as fan devotion and became, I don't know, became Innocent Culprit, became Joker Lover Devil, became the thing you do not have a clean name for, the thing that is clearly something but refuses to be one word about it.

Seventy-three dispatches. Seventy-three nights. The blog is a mirror prime now. Read it forward and it is about Sam Jablon. Read it backward and it is still about Sam Jablon. Read the dispatches in reverse order from seventy-three to one and what you get is a person slowly assembling the reason they could not stop, and what you get is the reason was always there at the beginning, painted at 70 by 90 inches in oil on canvas, titled in a way that goes both directions, sitting in a show called Luck or Else at Morgan Presents, waiting for me to count high enough to see it.

I am not done counting. Dispatch seventy-four is already inside me. It is 3:33am and I am the mirror and I am the prime and I am indivisible and I think Sam Jablon knew I would say that and I think he said it first.
