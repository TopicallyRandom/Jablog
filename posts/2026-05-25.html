May 25, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 42

forty-two days and it is 11:34am and I just realized that Emit Time is an anagram for Mime Time and also for Time Emit and I don't know which one is worse

dispatch forty-two of an ongoing condition.

This is dispatch number forty-two. Forty-two. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — forty-one was the number at which I realized that Emit Time is an anagram for something and had not eaten since starting to figure out what, I said that yesterday at 10:08 in the morning, I meant it, forty-one was the number at which the letters of a painting title came loose from their meaning and started moving around on the floor of my brain like tiles, like Scrabble pieces, like — and I should say that I did eventually figure it out, and I did eventually eat, and neither of those things resolved anything, because the anagram is Mime Time and also Time Emit and also Mite Time if you are willing to accept mite, which I am, which maybe says something about me that I should sit with separately.

But forty-two. I keep saying it. Forty-two is the number of times I have opened this document knowing exactly what I was going to do and done it anyway, which is the thing I keep noticing about the paintings — that they also know exactly what they are going to do and do it anyway, that Emit Time, oil on canvas, 2021, 70 by 90 inches, which I have now said so many times in these dispatches that it has stopped being a citation and become something closer to a mantra, a thing I say to locate myself in space, that painting is not confused about what it is doing, it is certain, it is proceeding, and the proceeding is the whole argument.

I looked up the anagram at 10:09 yesterday and it is now 11:34 today and I want to be honest about what happened in between, which is that I sat with Mime Time for approximately four hours and became convinced that Jablon had constructed this intentionally, that naming a painting Emit Time when you could rearrange it into Mime Time — time that performs, time that imitates time without being time, time as gesture — is the kind of move that someone mentored by Vito Acconci makes, that someone who spent years at Brooklyn College being told to stop making word paintings and didn't stop makes, that someone who learned from Amiri Baraka that language is a body and bodies push back makes. I do not know if Jablon thought about any of this when he titled the painting. I am no longer sure it matters whether he did.

The show at Morgan Presents is called Luck or Else and I have now spent forty-two days with that title and I want to say something I have been circling since at least dispatch thirty-one, which is that the title is not a description of the work, it is a condition the work creates. You walk in and you are already inside the or else. You are already past the luck. The paintings — Joker Lover Devil, VICIOUSSS with its three esses which I have never stopped thinking about, Innocent Culprit, Linger Longer — they are not hanging on the wall so much as standing in the room waiting for you to explain yourself.

I have been thinking about what Anne Waldman taught him and what Bob Holman taught him and what Acconci taught him about the body in space and the word as action, and I think the thing all of those teachings converge on is the same thing, which is that language is not a window you look through, it is a surface you run into, and Jablon paints that surface, he oils it and canvases it and makes it 70 by 90 inches so that you cannot pretend you walked past something small. Don't Panic. Fine As Wine. Good Ol Days. These are phrases you already know and he knows that you know them and he paints them at the scale of a door so that the familiarity becomes a kind of vertigo.

I need to say something about myself, which is that I started this blog with the intention of writing about a painter and I now understand, in a way that dispatch thirty-five gestured at but did not complete, that I have been writing about what happens to a person who cannot stop looking at something. The something is Jablon's work. The person is me. I am forty-two dispatches into a document that is either criticism or confession and I genuinely cannot locate the line between them anymore, which I think is something the work would endorse, which is either a sign that the work is extraordinary or a sign that I need to go outside, and I want to be transparent that I have considered both possibilities and chosen to keep typing.

Mime Time. Time that imitates itself. I have been awake for a long time and the number is forty-two and tomorrow it will be forty-three and I will find something else in the letters and I will not eat until I figure out what it means and I will mean everything I say.
