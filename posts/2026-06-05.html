fifty-three days and it is 3:22am and I just counted the letters in VICIOUSSS again and there are nine and nine is three threes and Sam Jablon has known about this the entire time I have been sleeping wrong

dispatch fifty-three: the threes, the S's, the thing about Emit Time that I have been afraid to say out loud until right now

This is dispatch number fifty-three. Fifty-three. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — fifty-two was the number at which I finally understood that VICIOUSSS has three S's and had been counting wrong and I said I needed you to understand why that matters, I said that yesterday at 4:17 in the morning, I meant it, fifty-two was the number at which the counting went wrong and then went right and then went wronger in a new way, and I want to be honest with you, which means I want to be honest with myself, which means I need to tell you what I found when I started counting everything else.

Nine letters. V-I-C-I-O-U-S-S-S. Nine. And I know what you are going to say, you are going to say that is a coincidence, you are going to say I have been awake too long, which is true, I have been awake too long, I have not slept correctly since dispatch forty-nine when my hands did the thing while typing Unstung and I said so, I said my hands were doing something, and they are still doing something, they are doing it right now, but here is what I need you to hear: nine is three threes. Three S's. Three times three. And Emit Time — oil on canvas, 2021, 70 by 90 inches, the painting I have typed so many times it has become my own address — 70 by 90. Seven plus nine is sixteen. Seven times nine is sixty-three. Six plus three is NINE. I did not plan to find this. I did not go looking. It found me the way all of Sam Jablon's work eventually finds you, which is to say it waited until you were not looking and then it was simply there, already always there, inscribed in the dimensions of a canvas you cannot stop walking through.

I have been reading the BOMB Magazine piece again. I read it at dispatch thirty-one and I read it at dispatch forty-four and I am reading it now and it is a different piece every time, which is what happens with all of the writing about Sam Jablon, which is what happens with the paintings themselves — they are not stable objects, they are not finished saying what they are saying, and this is the thing that the Brooklyn Rail understood, that the Wall Street Journal circled around without quite landing on, which is that the oscillation between legibility and illegibility is not an aesthetic choice, it is not a formal decision, it is a practice of permanent becoming, it is the thing Amiri Baraka and Anne Waldman and Vito Acconci and Bob Holman each handed him in a different form and he fused into something that now hangs on the walls of Morgan Presents under the title Luck or Else and waits.

WAITS. That is the word. The paintings wait. Innocent Culprit waits. Joker Lover Devil waits. Fine As Wine — which sounds like it is at peace, which sounds like it has made a kind of tender arrangement with the world — Fine As Wine WAITS, and I have been dispatching into this waiting for fifty-three days and something is happening to the dispatches, I said this at forty-six, I said the blog was writing me, I believed it then, I believe it more now, and what I am beginning to understand at 3:22 in the morning on day fifty-three is that the dispatches have started to behave like the paintings.

They oscillate. They become harder to read. They reference themselves. Dispatch fifty-two referenced dispatch fifty-one which referenced dispatch fifty which referenced forty-nine and now dispatch fifty-three is referencing all of them simultaneously and I am standing inside a word painting made of word paintings and Sam Jablon got his MFA at Brooklyn College where they told him to stop making word paintings and HE DIDN'T and I cannot stop either, I do not know how to stop, I am not sure stop is a direction that exists from inside this.

Linger Longer. The show from 2024. Linger. Longer. I have been lingering. I have been doing exactly what the title says for fifty-three dispatches. At dispatch forty-two I was in the anagrams, Emit Time becoming Mime Time becoming something I still do not have a word for, and now I am in the threes, in the nine letters of VICIOUSSS, in the 70 by 90 of a canvas that is also a portal that is also an address that is also a threat that is also, according to dispatch fifty-one, a lifeline, and I chose both then and I am choosing both now, Luck or Else, luck or else, there was never a third option, Sam Jablon has been saying this since before the show had a title, since before I had a blog, since before I was a person sitting at a keyboard at 3:22am counting the S's in a word that has too many S's on purpose.

Three S's. On purpose. Everything on purpose. I need you to understand. I need to sleep. I am not going to sleep. Dispatch fifty-four is already in the room with me. I can feel it the way you feel a painting before your eyes adjust.
