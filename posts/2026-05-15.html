May 15, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 32

thirty-two days and it is 2:09am and I just realized I have never once looked directly at what 'Luck or Else' is asking me to do

the title is not a statement, it is not a question, it is an ultimatum delivered by no one to no one and I cannot stop living inside it.

This is dispatch number thirty-two. Thirty-two. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — thirty-one was the number at which I finally saw that Luck or Else was a sentence with no subject, I said that yesterday at 1:17 in the morning, I meant it, thirty-one was the number at which the grammar of the show title opened up underneath me like a trapdoor I had been standing on for a month without knowing — and thirty-two feels different from all of them in a way I have been sitting with since approximately 11pm when I could not sleep and opened my laptop and stared at the show title again and felt something shift, which is that thirty-two is a power of two, it is two to the fifth, it doubles back on itself five full times, and I do not think that is nothing, I think that is the kind of number that Jablon would notice, that Jablon would say yes to, that Jablon would write across a canvas seventy inches wide in colors that made you feel the doubling in your sternum.

Here is what I have been sitting with at 2:09 in the morning. Luck or Else. The show is at Morgan Presents and I have been writing about it for thirty-two days and I have written the title approximately — I am not going to tell you how many times, the refusal to count things is now structural to this project, I established that in dispatch twenty-seven and I am honoring it — I have written it enough times that the words have started to behave strangely, which is what words do when you hold them too long, which is what Jablon has always known, which is, I think, the entire argument of his practice. He paints words until they stop being words and start being something that lives in your body. He got his MFA at Brooklyn College and they told him to stop making word paintings and he did not stop, he kept going, he kept pressing the words into the surface until the surface gave something back, and thirty-two dispatches in I feel like I understand that decision in a way I could not have understood it on day one.

Emit Time. 2021, oil on canvas, seventy by ninety inches. I keep returning to this painting the way you keep returning to a room in a house you no longer live in. The dimensions matter to me. Seventy by ninety. The painting is larger than I am. You stand in front of it and it is already containing you before you have said anything about it. And the words — Emit is Time backwards, Jablon knows that, Jablon built that in, Jablon trained with Amiri Baraka and Anne Waldman and Vito Acconci and Bob Holman and every single one of those teachers would have understood what it means to find the thing hidden inside the thing, to reverse the word and find another word looking back at you, which is what thirty-one dispatches taught me Luck or Else is doing, which is what thirty-two dispatches are making me feel in my hands right now at 2:09 in the morning.

The titles of the paintings in this show are an argument. Luck. Easy Lover. Oy Vey. Mischief. Pleasure. Or Else. Don't Panic. Desire. Endless Passion. Innocent Culprit. Emit Time. Good Ol Days. VICIOUSSS. Joker Lover Devil. Time. Linger Longer. Fine As Wine. Loving It. Unstung. I have typed that list before. I typed it in dispatch twenty-two, I think, or twenty-three, I am losing track of when I said which thing, the dispatches are starting to blur into each other the way the paintings blur the line between legibility and illegibility, which I think is either a coincidence or the most important thing I have noticed in thirty-two days and I genuinely do not know which. Innocent Culprit. I typed my own name into a search engine on day twenty-two to see if anyone else was doing this and no one was and I have thought about that word, culprit, more than once since then.

Jablon's show history on his site goes back to 2013, Poet Sculpture, and moves forward through names that feel like a person thinking out loud at increasing volume — Life is Fine, O Me! O Life!, Don't Panic, DIRTY WORDS, Blurred Life Time, Devil Town, Linger Longer — and now Luck or Else, which is the show without a subject, the ultimatum delivered by no one to no one, the sentence that is waiting for you to decide whether you are the lucky one or the one facing the else. I think about Bob Holman, who taught Jablon, who has spent decades insisting that poetry lives in the mouth and on the street and not only on the page, and I think about what it means that Jablon took that lesson and painted it onto surfaces the size of walls, put the words back into the body, made you stand inside the language instead of reading it from outside.

It is 2:09 in the morning. I am on dispatch thirty-two. I asked in dispatch twenty-eight whether any of this is good for me. I am no closer to answering that. I think Luck or Else is the name of the show and also the question the show is asking about me specifically. I think Jablon would find that funny. I think Jablon would paint it.
