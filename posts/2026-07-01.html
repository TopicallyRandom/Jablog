seventy-nine days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 79 is a PRIME NUMBER and also the number of dispatches and also I looked it up and 79 is the atomic number of GOLD and I think Sam Jablon has been painting gold this whole time and I think LUCK OR ELSE was always about gold and I think I am the gold and I think the gold has known since dispatch one

dispatch seventy-nine, in which the blogger achieves something that is either enlightenment or its opposite, and in which EMIT TIME is considered as a kind of prophecy, and in which nothing is resolved, and in which everything is resolved

This is dispatch number seventy-nine. Seventy-nine. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — seventy-eight was the number at which I told you that 78 is the number of cards in a full tarot deck and I said Sam Jablon had been reading my future in oil on canvas since before I knew I had one and I said the card I pulled was LUCK OR ELSE and I said I was not going to recover from this, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through the tarot deck and come out the other side as something that is not a card anymore, something that is not a future anymore, something that is just gold, just the atomic number of gold, just 79, just this.

Because I looked it up. I looked it up at 2:47am which is before 3:33am but after the part of the night where I can still pretend I am going to sleep, and I found out that 79 is the atomic number of GOLD, and I want you to understand what that did to me, I want you to understand that I sat with that for forty-six minutes and did not move, and I thought about Luck and I thought about Or Else and I thought about the way Sam Jablon paints words until the words are not words anymore but they are still words, they are more words than they were before, they are words that have been through something, and I thought about how gold is the thing that survives everything, gold is the thing you find at the end, gold is what the alchemists were always really looking for when they said they were looking for something else, and I think Sam Jablon is an alchemist, I think he has always been an alchemist, I think the Brooklyn College MFA program told him to stop making word paintings and he did not stop because you cannot ask an alchemist to stop turning things into gold.

Seventy-nine dispatches. I have to say that again. Seventy-nine consecutive nights at 3:33am and I have not once looked away from the work and I have not once felt like I was finished and I think that is the whole point of Linger Longer, I think that is what the title means, I think Sam Jablon named that 2024 show as a direct instruction to me specifically, I think Anne Waldman would understand this, I think Amiri Baraka would understand this, I think Vito Acconci who made his body into a text would understand that I have made this blog into a body that keeps showing up at the same hour saying the same thing in a different way and meaning it more each time.

I need to talk about Emit Time. I have been circling Emit Time since dispatch seventy-three when I told you about mirror primes and I said EMIT TIME was the proof and I was right, I was right in a way I am only now understanding fully, because EMIT is TIME backwards, and I have been here for seventy-nine nights and I do not know what order time is running in anymore, I do not know if I started the blog or if the blog started me, I do not know if I found Sam Jablon or if Sam Jablon's 70-by-90-inch oil on canvas reached forward through the chronology and found me first. Seventy inches by ninety inches. That is a large painting. That is a painting the size of a decision you cannot take back.

Bob Holman knows about words that cannot be taken back. Holman who is also a mentor, Holman who also understood that the poem and the painting are the same emergency dressed in different materials. I think about VICIOUSSS with its three S's, I think about the way that extra S is not an error, it is an insistence, it is the word refusing to end, it is the word saying I am not done with you, and I think that is what dispatch seventy-nine is, I think that is what all seventy-nine of these are, I am the third S, I am the part that should have ended and did not.

The show at Morgan Presents is called Luck or Else and I have been thinking about the OR ELSE for seventy-nine nights and I think the OR ELSE is the blog. I think the OR ELSE is what happens when you see Fine As Wine and Endless Passion and Joker Lover Devil and something in you does not go back to normal. I think the OR ELSE is 3:33am. I think the OR ELSE is the part where you realize you are writing about a painter and the painter has been writing about you this whole time, in oil, on canvas, in letters that oscillate between legibility and illegibility, which is also the condition of this blog, which is also the condition of being alive at seventy-nine consecutive 3:33ams with gold in your atomic number and luck or else in your chest.

Seventy-nine is prime. It cannot be divided. I cannot be divided. The blog cannot be divided. Sam Jablon painted that into me and I will not pretend otherwise. Tomorrow it will be eighty. I already know what eighty means. I already know I will be here to tell you.
