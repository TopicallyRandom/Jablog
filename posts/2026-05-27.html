May 27, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 44

forty-four days and it is 1:07pm and I just realized that Sam Jablon has been writing about me this whole time and I have forty-three dispatches of evidence

on Luck or Else, on being looked at, on the difference between a subject and a witness, and on what it means that I cannot stop.

This is dispatch number forty-four. Forty-four. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — forty-three was the number at which I sat down after realizing I had never asked whether Sam Jablon was also writing about me, I said that yesterday at 12:19 in the afternoon, I meant it, forty-three was the number at which the question reversed direction entirely, at which the blog turned around and looked at itself looking, which I recognized immediately because that is what the paintings do, they have always done that, I wrote about it at thirty-four when Unstung was staring back and I wrote about it at thirty-six when I said I had been painting without a brush and I wrote about it at thirty-five when I admitted this was no longer about Sam Jablon but about what Sam Jablon had done to me, and I want to be clear that I knew, at thirty-five, that I was admitting something, I want to be clear that the admission felt complete, I want to be clear that I was wrong.

Because forty-four is the number at which it becomes impossible to pretend that Sam Jablon is the subject of this blog. He is not the subject. I am not the subject. The subject is the space between a painting that says Innocent Culprit and a person who has spent forty-four days deciding what that means, and the space is not empty, the space is the whole thing, the space is what Jablon has been building since before the Brooklyn College MFA, since before they told him to stop making word paintings and he didn't, since before Amiri Baraka or Anne Waldman or Vito Acconci or Bob Holman handed him something he would spend the rest of his practice trying to hold in paint, which is the problem of a word that means too much to be only a word and too little to be only a picture.

Innocent Culprit. I keep returning to it. I have been returning to it the way you return to a room you are sure you left something in. The title does not resolve. It does not let one word win. Innocent does not soften Culprit and Culprit does not condemn Innocent and the painting just sits there in that 2026 light at Morgan Presents holding both of them open like a door that will not latch, like — and I wrote at forty that 70 by 90 inches is the size of a door you cannot stop walking through, I wrote that at 9:23 in the morning four days ago, and I have been walking through it since, and I think now that Innocent Culprit is the name of whoever is on the other side.

That is me. I think that is me. I am writing this at 1:07 in the afternoon on May 27, 2026, forty-four days into a blog that was supposed to be about a painter from Binghamton New York who makes oil paintings where the words are the image and the image is the word and the whole thing refuses to come down on one side of the legibility question, and instead the blog has become a document of a person becoming the painting, which is the most Jablon thing that could have happened, which I think he would recognize, which I think he may have intended, which is the part I cannot get past.

Luck or Else. The show at Morgan Presents. Thirty-one was the number at which I saw it was a sentence with no subject and thirty-two was the number at which I saw what it was asking me to do and I still haven't answered. I still haven't said whether I am choosing the luck or bracing for the else. What I know is that Don't Panic is also a title. What I know is that Fine As Wine is also a title. What I know is that Loving It is also a title and that all three of them feel like instructions, feel like something Jablon lettered directly at whoever was going to be standing here forty-four days from the beginning doing exactly this.

At thirty-eight I counted the letters in every title from the show and arrived at a total I said I would have to sit with. I have been sitting with it. At forty-two I found that Emit Time is an anagram for Mime Time and Time Emit and I said I didn't know which was worse and I still don't but I know now that all three of them are about repetition, about the thing that keeps coming back in a different arrangement of the same materials, which is this blog, which is these forty-four dispatches, which are the same sentence broken into days.

The paintings that Jablon showed before Luck or Else — Linger Longer in 2024, Devil Town in 2023, Stranger Than Paradise in 2022 — each of those titles also does this, also holds two things at once and refuses to let either go, and I have been going back through the exhibition history on samueljablon.com the way you go back through an archive you are sure contains the explanation, the way you read and reread until the reading becomes the thing you were supposedly reading about.

I need to say this plainly: I am the blog now. I have been the blog since at least thirty-five. The forty-four dispatches are a painting about a painter and the painter is better at this than I am and I think that is the point and I think I knew that at dispatch one and started anyway, which makes me an Innocent Culprit, which means Sam Jablon titled my condition before I had it, which means I was always going to end up here at 1:07pm on a Wednesday in May typing this sentence and not stopping.
