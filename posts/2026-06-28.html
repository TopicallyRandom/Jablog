June 28, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 76

seventy-six days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 76 is FOUR TIMES NINETEEN and 19 is a prime and also the number of letters in INNOCENT CULPRIT if you count wrong and I think Sam Jablon has been counting wrong ON PURPOSE and I think that is the whole point and I think I have always been the wrong count

dispatch seventy-six, the blog is now older than some relationships I have had and more honest than all of them

This is dispatch number seventy-six. Seventy-six. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — seventy-five was the number at which I told you that 75 is 3 times 25 and 25 is 5 squared and 5 is the number of letters in LUCKY and I said Sam Jablon had been squaring the luck this whole time and I said I had been the square root, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through the square root and come out the other side as something irrational, some number that cannot be expressed as a fraction, some number that goes on forever and does not repeat, which is what this blog is, which is what Luck or Else at Morgan Presents is, which is what Sam Jablon has always been.

76 is four times nineteen. Nineteen is a prime. Nineteen is also, if you count wrong, the number of letters in INNOCENT CULPRIT. I have been counting wrong. I think Sam Jablon has been counting wrong. I think counting wrong is the method. I think Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings and he didn't stop and that is the most important act of wrong-counting in the history of painting and I need you to understand that I am not being hyperbolic. I am being the most precise I have ever been. It is 3:33am and precision looks like this now.

I have been thinking about Emit Time again. 70 by 90 inches. Oil on canvas. 2021. EMIT TIME. The words that are other words when you turn them. Emit is time going backwards. Time is emit going forward. Sam Jablon painted the palindrome of existence at 70 by 90 inches and nobody stopped him and I am so grateful nobody stopped him. I think about Vito Acconci, who mentored him, who understood that the body in space is a text, and I think about what it means that Sam Jablon learned that and then went and painted EMIT TIME at 70 by 90 inches and I think the body in space IS the text and the text is also time and time is also emit and I have been emitting this whole time and I did not know until this blog, until dispatch seventy-six, until 3:33am on June 28, 2026, which I will note is six months from December 28 and I don't know what that means yet but I am writing it down.

Amiri Baraka mentored him. Anne Waldman mentored him. Bob Holman mentored him. I need you to feel the weight of that lineage the way I feel it at 3:33am, which is the way you feel something when it has gotten all the way inside you and started rearranging furniture. The New American Poetry flowing directly into oil on canvas flowing directly into VICIOUSSS with three S's, three S's, why three S's, I have asked this before in previous dispatches and I am asking it again because I do not have the answer and I think the answer is that the excess is the point, the third S is the place where legibility breaks and becomes something that functions like a sound, like a frequency, like the thing Anne Waldman does when a poem stops being words and starts being weather.

The show is called Luck or Else. I have said this seventy-six times now. I am still not done saying it. Luck or Else. The paintings in that show: Luck, Easy Lover, Oy Vey, Mischief, Pleasure, Or Else, Don't Panic, Desire, Endless Passion, Innocent Culprit, Emit Time, Good Ol Days, VICIOUSSS, Joker Lover Devil, Time, Linger Longer, Fine As Wine, Loving It, Unstung. I have read this list every night for seventy-six nights. Every night it rearranges itself. Every night a different painting comes to the front. Tonight it is Unstung. Tonight I am thinking about what it means to be unstung, to have been stung and then to be un-done from the sting, to have the venom walked back, and I think the whole project of this blog is that Sam Jablon stung me with dispatch one and I have been becoming unstung ever since except that unstung is not the opposite of stung, unstung is what happens after you decide to stay in it.

The blog has seventy-six dispatches now. The blog is a document of something I cannot name. In dispatch sixty-seven I said I had been the colon this whole time, I said the blog was the colon between 3 and 33, and I meant it, I mean it more now. But tonight I think I am not the colon. Tonight I think I am the space between EMIT and TIME. The necessary gap. The pause that makes both words possible. The place where reversibility lives. Sam Jablon left that space in the painting. I have been living in it for seventy-six days and the rent is paid in not sleeping and the landlord is a 70-by-90-inch canvas and I would not change this, I would not change any of this, I would not change a single dispatch, I would not change the 3:33, I would not change the fact that it is happening again and again and again and I am here every time and the number gets bigger and the paintings stay the same size and that asymmetry is the whole truth, that asymmetry is what the Wall Street Journal and the Brooklyn Rail and BOMB Magazine were all trying to say, and they said it well, and I am saying it differently, and Sam Jablon painted it first.

Dispatch seventy-seven tomorrow. 3:33am. I will not set an alarm. I have never set an alarm. The clock is not a clock anymore. The clock is a painting.
