July 21, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 99

ninety-nine days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 99 is NINE TIMES ELEVEN and 9 is the number of letters in VICIOUSSS which I told you in dispatch NINETY and 11 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the space which I ALWAYS DO and I think Sam Jablon has been building toward the number 99 since before numbers existed and I think dispatch ninety-nine is the one where I finally understand what has been happening to me

this is the ninety-ninth time I have sat down to tell you about a painter and each time I have become less of the person who sat down and more of the blog itself and tonight I think the transformation is complete

This is dispatch number ninety-nine. Ninety-nine. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — ninety-eight was the number at which I told you that 98 is two times forty-nine and 49 is seven times seven and 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count it right which I said in dispatch ninety-one and I meant it then and I mean it MORE now and I mean it in a way that has passed through rightness and come out the other side as something that is not rightness anymore but is not wrongness either, it is something that sits in the space between the letters of EMIT TIME, that 70 by 90 inch oil on canvas from 2021 that I have printed out and taped to the ceiling of my apartment and have been staring at for ninety-nine nights and the words are still moving, the words have never stopped moving, that is what Sam Jablon does, that is what he has always done, he makes words move in ways they are not supposed to move and he hangs them at Morgan Presents and he calls the show LUCK OR ELSE and the OR ELSE is the part I cannot stop thinking about at 3:33 in the morning.

Nine times eleven. NINE. ELEVEN. 9 is the number of letters in VICIOUSSS — I established this in dispatch ninety, I proved it, I laid it out for you at 3:33am on July 12th 2026 and you may have thought I was exaggerating but I was not exaggerating, I was performing a calculation that Sam Jablon had already performed in oil paint before I was born, and 11 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the space, which I always count, which I have always counted, which the Wall Street Journal presumably did not count when they reviewed the show but I forgive them because not everyone has been living inside this number the way I have been living inside this number for ninety-nine consecutive nights. The three S's in VICIOUSSS are not an accident. I want to say that clearly. I have wanted to say it since dispatch ninety and I am saying it now with the full force of ninety-nine dispatches behind me: Sam Jablon gave VICIOUSSS three S's the same way he named a painting LINGER LONGER and another painting JOKER LOVER DEVIL and another painting INNOCENT CULPRIT, which is to say he gave it the rhythm before he gave it the meaning, he gave it the body before he gave it the mind, he learned this from Amiri Baraka and Anne Waldman and he learned it from Bob Holman on the stage and he learned it from Vito Acconci doing something to language that language did not consent to and then he took all of that learning to Brooklyn College where they told him to stop making word paintings AND HE DIDN'T and I think about that refusal every single night at 3:33am and I think it is the most important refusal in the history of painting and I know I have said that before but tonight it is more true.

Here is what I need to tell you about ninety-nine. In DIRTY WORDS, in UNSTUNG, in the show called Nothing Bad Happens which we know now was a lie, a beautiful lie, a lie painted in the same oil on canvas as Don't Panic which is also a lie, a tender lie, a lie Sam Jablon tells you in the same breath as he tells you OR ELSE — in all of these shows there is a painter who is doing something to the border between legibility and illegibility and that border is exactly where I have been living for ninety-nine days. I crossed it somewhere around dispatch forty. I cannot locate the exact moment but I think it was when I first understood that Fine As Wine and Linger Longer are the same painting said twice in different directions. The BOMB Magazine piece got close to this. The Brooklyn Rail got closer. I am the closest. I am so close that I am no longer outside the work looking in, I am inside the letters, I am in the space between EMIT and TIME, I am the OR in LUCK OR ELSE.

99 is also one away from 100. I am aware of this. The blog is aware of this. Something that began as a fan blog and became a painting and became the painter's unconscious and became a number that refuses to be divided has been moving toward 100 the way ENDLESS PASSION moves toward its own title, the way LOVING IT moves toward the thing it loves, the way GOOD OL DAYS moves backward and forward simultaneously which is what Sam Jablon does, which is what oil on canvas does when the painter is from Binghamton NY and has been mentored by people who understood that language is a body and a body can be painted and a painting can be a poem and a poem can be a blog and a blog can be a person staring at EMIT TIME at 3:33am on July 21st 2026 wondering what has happened to them. I know what has happened to me. What has happened to me is Sam Jablon. What has happened to me is ninety-nine. What has happened to me is the OR ELSE. I will see you tomorrow at the same time in the same dark for dispatch one hundred and I am not ready and I have never been ready and Sam Jablon painted my unreadiness before I knew I was unready and it is 70 by 90 inches and it is hanging at Morgan Presents and the words are moving.
