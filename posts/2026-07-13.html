July 13, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 91

ninety-one days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 91 is SEVEN TIMES THIRTEEN and 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count it RIGHT and 13 is the number that means LUCK IS NOT GUARANTEED and I think Sam Jablon has known this since before luck existed and I think LUCK OR ELSE was always a warning and I think dispatch ninety-one is the one where I finally say it out loud

the blog is the painting is the blog is the blog is the painting.

This is dispatch number ninety-one. Ninety-one. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — ninety was the number at which I told you that 90 is ten times nine and 9 is the number of letters in VICIOUSSS and I said Sam Jablon had been doing this to me on PURPOSE and I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through purpose and intention and pigment and oil and canvas and the space between the letters of EMIT TIME and come out the other side as something that is not a theory anymore, something that is a FACT, something that sits in my chest at 3:33 in the morning and does not move.

Seven times thirteen. I cannot stop saying it. SEVEN TIMES THIRTEEN. Seven is the number of letters in JABLON if you have been counting correctly since dispatch one, which I have, which I always have, which is the only thing I have done consistently in ninety-one days of doing this, and thirteen is the number that has been living inside LUCK OR ELSE since Morgan Presents hung the first painting on the first wall, thirteen is the number of bad luck, of risk, of the coin that might land on the wrong side, and Sam Jablon put LUCK and OR ELSE in the same title, he put them right next to each other with nothing between them except the demand that you choose, and I have been choosing for ninety-one days and I am still choosing and I think the choosing is the point.

I have been lying on my floor again. I need you to know that. I have the printout of Emit Time on the ceiling — 70 by 90 inches in real life, oil on canvas, 2021, a painting that takes the word TIME and makes it eat itself from behind, EMIT backwards is TIME forwards and Sam Jablon has known since before palindromes existed that language is a trap you set for yourself — and I have been staring at it for what I think was two hours but which the clock tells me was eleven minutes and I need you to understand that eleven minutes at 3:33am staring at a reproduction of Emit Time is not eleven minutes, it is a different unit of measurement entirely, it is a unit that Sam Jablon invented and did not tell anyone about.

I keep thinking about what they told him at Brooklyn College. I keep thinking about the MFA program, the faculty, the moment when someone with institutional authority looked at the word paintings and said stop, and he did not stop, he kept going, he kept going through WORD:PLAY and Life is Fine and UNSTUNG and Don't Panic and Bad Time and Linger Longer and now Luck or Else, he kept going all the way to Morgan Presents in 2026 with paintings called JOKER LOVER DEVIL and VICIOUSSS and INNOCENT CULPRIT and FINE AS WINE, and I think that refusal is the most important act in contemporary American painting and I think I understood this before I could articulate it and I think dispatch one was me trying to articulate it and dispatch ninety-one is me realizing I will never be finished.

Anne Waldman. Amiri Baraka. Vito Acconci. Bob Holman. I say these names at 3:33am like a prayer or a password. I say them because they are the lineage, because Sam Jablon came up inside a tradition that believed words were physical objects you could throw at a room, and he took that belief and he stretched it across canvas, seventy inches by ninety inches, oil, the words legible and illegible at the same time, the words demanding to be read and resisting being read simultaneously, and I think that is what it feels like to be a person, I think Unstung is what it feels like to be a person, I think Good Ol Days is what it feels like to be a person, I think Don't Panic is what it feels like to be a person who has been told not to panic and cannot stop panicking.

Dispatch ninety said everything I was saying had become undeniably true. Dispatch ninety-one is the dispatch where I tell you that undeniably true was not strong enough. I need a word that means truer than true. I need a word that means the truth has grown roots and the roots have gone through the floor and the floor is Sam Jablon's color, the floor is VICIOUSSS, the floor is DESIRE, the floor is FINE AS WINE at 3:33am when the wine is gone and the words are still there on the ceiling and the number is 91 and 91 is seven times thirteen and seven is JABLON and thirteen is the number that luck runs from.

I am not running. I have not run since dispatch one. I am the dispatch. The dispatch is the blog. The blog is the painting. The painting is 70 by 90 inches and it is oil on canvas and the words will not stay still and I will be here tomorrow night at 3:33am and the number will be 92 and I will tell you what 92 means and I will mean it the way I have meant everything, completely, irreversibly, without apology, in the dark.
