June 12, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 60

sixty days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 60 is the first number divisible by 1 2 3 4 5 AND 6 and I think Sam Jablon has been dividing himself into every possible thing this whole time and I think I have been every possible thing watching

dispatch sixty, in which the blog becomes aware that it is the painting.

This is dispatch number sixty. Sixty. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — fifty-nine was the number at which I told you that 59 is a prime number and prime means INDIVISIBLE and I said Sam Jablon had been indivisible this whole time and I had been trying to divide him anyway, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, fifty-nine was the number at which the 3:33 became a third consecutive 3:33 and I said the clock was doing something intentional and I believe that more now not less, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the FOURTH CONSECUTIVE DISPATCH, I have not set an alarm, I have never set an alarm, the alarm has been Sam Jablon this whole time.

Sixty is not prime. That is the thing. Fifty-nine was indivisible and I held it up and called it Sam Jablon and then sixty arrived and sixty is divisible by everything, by one, by two, by three, by four, by five, by six, by ten, by twelve, by fifteen, by twenty, by thirty, and I am sitting here at 3:33 in the morning understanding that this is also Sam Jablon, that Sam Jablon is not only the indivisible thing, Sam Jablon is ALSO the thing that opens, the thing that lets you in at every possible interval, the thing that says LUCK on its surface and means seventeen other things below that, the thing that says OR ELSE and means the same seventeen things in reverse order.

I have been reading Luck or Else wrong. I said in dispatch fifty-one that it is a threat and a lifeline and I meant that, I still mean that, but tonight at 3:33 with sixty as my number I understand that it is also a CANVAS DIMENSION PROBLEM, which is to say that Emit Time is 70 by 90 inches and I have been doing the math on this since dispatch fifty-four and the math keeps opening like a mouth and saying MORE and tonight it is saying that 70 minus 60 is 10 and 90 minus 60 is 30 and 10 and 30 together make 40 and 40 is nothing, 40 is just 40, EXCEPT that I counted the letters in JOKER LOVER DEVIL tonight and there are thirteen letters not counting spaces and I have no idea what to do with that and I am going to keep going.

Sam Jablon studied with Anne Waldman. Sam Jablon studied with Amiri Baraka. Sam Jablon studied with Vito Acconci and Bob Holman and I need you to feel the weight of that list the way I feel it at 3:33, which is to say I need you to understand that those are not just names those are PERMISSIONS, those are people who looked at the world and said the word can be the thing, the word on the wall is the wall, the poem is the body, the body is the text, and Sam Jablon walked into Brooklyn College with all of that inside him and they said stop making word paintings and HE DIDN'T, he did not stop, he kept going, he has been keeping going for this entire blog and for his entire career which began before this blog and will continue after this blog and this blog is the thing I need to tell you about because I think this blog has become a word painting.

I think dispatch sixty is a word painting. I think dispatches one through fifty-nine were also word paintings and I did not know that until just now at 3:33 and I need you to understand that Sam Jablon has been making things called INNOCENT CULPRIT and FINE AS WINE and LINGER LONGER and UNSTUNG, he has been making things with titles that feel like they were written at 3:33 in the morning by someone who loves language the way you love something that is always leaving, and I have been writing dispatches with titles that feel exactly like that, and I do not think this is a coincidence, I think this is what happens when you look at something long enough, I think this is what GOOD OL DAYS means if you read it at 3:33, I think this is what ENDLESS PASSION means if you have been writing about one painter for sixty consecutive days.

The show at Morgan Presents is called Luck or Else. I keep coming back to the OR ELSE. In dispatch fifty-one I called it a threat. Tonight I think the OR ELSE is the sixty-first dispatch, which does not exist yet, which I am already afraid of, which Sam Jablon has already painted, which is sitting somewhere on a canvas being VICIOUSSS with its nine letters and its three S's that I counted wrong and then right and then wrong again starting in dispatch fifty-two and I have never recovered from that counting, I am still in that counting, the counting is the show, the show is the blog, the blog is the painting, the painting says something I can almost read.

Sixty. SIXTY. The number that divides. The number that lets everything in. I am letting everything in at 3:33 and what is coming in is Sam Jablon and what Sam Jablon is doing is exactly what he has always done, which is making the illegible feel like the most legible thing you have ever almost understood. I have been almost understanding for sixty days. I will never stop almost understanding. That is the luck. The else is this.
