fifty-four days and it is 3:41am and I just realized that Emit Time is 70 by 90 inches and I need to tell you what that means because I think the canvas itself is a clock and I think it has been running this whole time

dispatch fifty-four, in which the dimensions become a problem I cannot stop doing arithmetic about.

This is dispatch number fifty-four. Fifty-four. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — fifty-three was the number at which I counted the letters in VICIOUSSS again and there were nine and nine is three threes and I said Sam Jablon had known about this the entire time I have been sleeping wrong, I said that yesterday at 3:22 in the morning, I meant it, fifty-three was the number at which the counting went wrong and then went right and then went wronger in a new way, and I want to be honest with you, which means I want to be honest with myself, which means I need to tell you about 70 by 90.

Seventy by ninety. Seventy by ninety inches. This is the size of Emit Time, 2021, oil on canvas, and I have known this fact for a long time but I did not look at it until 3:41 this morning which is when something cracked open in me that I do not have the clinical vocabulary to describe and so I will just say: seventy divided by ninety is 0.777 repeating and seven is the number of letters in JABLON S and I need you to understand I did not plan to do that math I just did it the way you breathe. The sevens keep coming. The sevens have always been coming. Sam Jablon has been making paintings about time and he made the time painting 70 by 90 and the 70 is all sevens and I am not saying this is intentional I am saying intentional is the wrong category.

Here is what I know about Emit Time. I know it is a title that goes in two directions simultaneously and I know that this is not a trick, it is a structural commitment, it is the same commitment Sam Jablon has been making since WORD:PLAY in 2014 when he decided that language could be load-bearing in a painting the same way a wall is load-bearing in a building, which means if you remove it the whole thing falls, which means it was never decoration, which means Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings and he looked at whoever said that and he kept going and I have to lie down when I think about this. I cannot always not lie down.

The mentors. I keep coming back to the mentors. Anne Waldman. Amiri Baraka. Vito Acconci. Bob Holman. I have written this list before and every time I write it it feels different, it feels like a different list, it feels like the list is Emit Time, it feels like it runs in two directions, like Waldman-Baraka-Acconci-Holman is also Holman-Acconci-Baraka-Waldman and both lists produce Sam Jablon at the center holding a brush and choosing to paint words when everyone around him had opinions about that. EVERYONE AROUND HIM HAD OPINIONS. He has the opinions now. The opinions are 70 by 90 inches and they are in oil and they will outlast all of us including me and I find this comforting and also devastating in equal measure.

Dispatch forty-two I found the anagram. Emit Time is an anagram for Mime Time and also for Time Emit and I said I did not know which one was worse and I still do not know but I know now that the painting is also 70 by 90 and so the anagram lives inside a rectangle that is almost but not quite a square and that almost-but-not-quite is doing something, that is not nothing, the canvas shape is a near-miss the way all of Sam Jablon's titles are near-misses, they almost say one thing and then they say the other thing, Luck or Else, which I have now understood in at least four completely different ways across four consecutive sleepless dispatches and which is currently showing at Morgan Presents and which I have not visited in person because I am not sure I would survive it.

I said in dispatch fifty-one that Luck or Else is a threat and also a lifeline and also the only two options. I stand by this. I would tattoo this on my arm if I were a different person. I am not a different person. I am the person who is awake at 3:41am doing arithmetic about canvas dimensions and feeling, genuinely feeling, that Sam Jablon anticipated this moment, that Emit Time was painted in 2021 knowing that in 2026 someone would be sitting here doing exactly this math. The Linger Longer show was 2024. The Devil Town show was 2023. The timeline is a painting. The painting is a timeline. Emit time. Time emits. The blog has been writing me since dispatch forty-six said so and I did not disagree then and I do not disagree now.

It is 3:58am now. Seventeen minutes have passed since I started this sentence. Seventeen. I am going to leave that there and I am not going to explain it and Sam Jablon would understand why.
