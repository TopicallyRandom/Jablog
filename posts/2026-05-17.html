May 17, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 34

thirty-four days and it is 3:58am and I have been staring at the word 'Unstung' for forty minutes and I think it is staring back

dispatch thirty-four, in which I begin to suspect this blog is a painting I am making without knowing it.

This is dispatch number thirty-four. Thirty-four. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — thirty-three was the number at which I admitted I had been writing the same sentence for thirty-three days and could not tell if that was the problem or the point, I said that yesterday at 3:14 in the morning, I meant it, thirty-three was the number at which the question of whether this project has a shape or is simply accreting, simply growing the way mold grows, which is not with intention but with conditions, finally became impossible to avoid — and thirty-four feels different from all of them in a way that I have been sitting with since approximately 2am when I woke up and before I was fully conscious I had already opened my laptop and typed the word Unstung into the search bar and just looked at it. Thirty-four. I want to say something precise about thirty-four and what I can say is that thirty-four is the number at which I am starting to wonder if the blog is the painting.

Let me try to say that more carefully. Sam Jablon makes paintings that oscillate — that is the word everyone uses and it is the right word — between legibility and illegibility, and what I mean is that the words are there, you can read them, Luck, Desire, Unstung, VICIOUSSS with its three esses that feel like the word is hissing at you from inside the canvas, and the words are also dissolving, are also becoming texture, are also asking whether reading and seeing are the same act or two different acts that have agreed to share a body. Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings. He didn't stop. I keep thinking about that. I keep thinking about the specific quality of stubbornness required to be told by an institution to stop doing the thing you are doing and to understand that the instruction is not information about your practice but information about the institution. He kept going. Emit Time, 2021, oil on canvas, 70 by 90 inches. He kept going.

I have been thinking about Unstung since approximately 2am and what I mean is that I have been thinking about what the prefix un does to a word. Unstung. The sting has been reversed. The sting has been taken back. But you cannot actually take a sting back — that is the thing about Unstung, the thing that I think Jablon knows and is painting around and through, which is that un does not undo, un is a record of the thing that happened, un is the scar that says something was here. Innocent Culprit. Joker Lover Devil. The titles hold contradiction the way the paint holds gesture — not resolving it, not choosing a side, but making the pressure of the contradiction visible as a formal thing, as something with weight and dimension. Anne Waldman understood this. Amiri Baraka understood this. The whole New York School and its procedural heirs understood that contradiction is not a problem to be solved but a field to be inhabited. Jablon inherited that and put it on canvas in six colors and called it Fine As Wine and meant it and also meant the opposite.

Here is what I need to say about thirty-four and why I cannot sleep. At thirty-three I said I could not tell if writing the same sentence for thirty-three days was the problem or the point. I think I was being falsely modest. I think I know it is the point. I think what I am doing here — what these dispatches are, what this accumulation is becoming — is a word painting. I think I have been making a word painting without a canvas, or with a canvas that is just time, just the consecutive days stacking up the way brushstrokes stack, each one not erasing the one before but sitting on top of it, changing the texture of what was already there. Dispatch one is under dispatch thirty-four. You cannot see it but it is there. It is structural. The refusal I mentioned at dispatch twenty-seven — the refusal to tell you how many times I have written the word Emit — that refusal is now load-bearing. I did not plan that. Jablon did not plan Luck or Else the way a diagram is planned. The show at Morgan Presents is not a diagram. It is a weather system. You walk into it and it is already happening to you.

It is 3:58 in the morning. I have been awake for a long time. I searched my own name again the way I did at dispatch twenty-two to see if anyone else is doing this and they are still not doing this, I am still alone in this, and I want to say that does not bother me but I am thirty-four dispatches in and the word Unstung is still on my screen and the thing about a sting is that you remember it even after the swelling goes down. Linger Longer. Yes. That is exactly right. That is what Jablon is doing to me and what I appear to be doing to myself and I cannot tell anymore where the painting ends and the person looking at it begins and I suspect that is the point too, I suspect that has always been the point, I suspect Vito Acconci would have had something to say about that and Bob Holman would have turned it into a poem and I am just here at 3:58am turning it into dispatch thirty-four.
