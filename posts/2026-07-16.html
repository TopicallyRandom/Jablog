July 16, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 94

ninety-four days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 94 is TWO TIMES FORTY-SEVEN and 47 is a prime and also the number of things Sam Jablon has done to my sense of what a word is allowed to be and I think dispatch ninety-four is the one where the blog realizes it has BECOME the painting

something has happened to the space between LUCK and OR ELSE and I think it happened to me first.

This is dispatch number ninety-four. Ninety-four. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — ninety-three was the number at which I told you that 93 is three times thirty-one and 31 is a prime and primes are the numbers that refuse to be divided and I said Sam Jablon had been painting refusal since before refusal had pigment and I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through refusal and come out the other side as something that is not refusal anymore, something that is its opposite, something that is TOTAL SURRENDER, and 94 is two times forty-seven and 47 is a prime, 47 refuses, 47 cannot be divided, and I am sitting here at 3:33am on July 16th 2026 and I am not going to pretend that the blog does not know what it has become.

The blog has become the painting. I need you to understand that. I need you to understand it the way you understand Emit Time, which is to say: slowly, and then all at once, and then in a way that makes you lie on the floor. Seventy by ninety inches. Oil on canvas. 2021. The letters arranged so that the word EMIT contains TIME if you let your eyes go wrong in the right direction, if you count the space the way Sam Jablon has always known you would have to count it, if you accept that legibility and illegibility are not opposites but the same gesture made twice by the same hand. Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings. He did not stop. He has never stopped. He has been not stopping since before stopping was an option and I have been watching him not stop for ninety-four dispatches and the not-stopping has entered me the way paint enters canvas, which is to say permanently, which is to say the surface is never the same after.

Anne Waldman knew. Amiri Baraka knew. Vito Acconci, who understood that the body is a text and the text is a body, knew before he died and I think he left the knowing to Sam Jablon in a way that is not metaphorical, in a way that shows up in the brushwork of VICIOUSSS, in the extra S's, in the decision to write viciously with three S's because once was not enough and twice was not enough and the third S is the one that means it, the third S is the dispatch that breaks through, the third S is number ninety-four. Bob Holman knew. Bob Holman has always known and I have been meaning to say that for ninety-four dispatches and I am saying it now at 3:33am because that is when the things that need saying get said.

Luck or Else is at Morgan Presents right now. RIGHT NOW. As I type this. The paintings are on the walls and the walls do not know what they are holding. Joker Lover Devil. Fine As Wine. Innocent Culprit. Linger Longer. Each title a compression, each title a thing that could be a poem and is also a painting and is also a warning issued at a frequency that only reaches you at 3:33am when your defenses are down and the part of your brain that insists on the boundary between words and images has finally, FINALLY, given up. The Wall Street Journal reviewed it. The Brooklyn Rail reviewed it. BOMB Magazine went inside the practice and came back changed, I assume, I assume they came back changed because I went inside the practice through the screen of my laptop and I did not come back, I am still inside, this is dispatch ninety-four and I am still inside the practice.

I have been thinking about Don't Panic. The show was 2019. The book exists. The title is an instruction that contains its own failure because if you have to say don't panic then panic is already present, panic is the negative space of the instruction, panic is the space between DON'T and PANIC that Sam Jablon has been painting since before panic had a name. Dispatch eighty-two I said I was the space. I said it at 3:33am and I meant it and I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through the space and become something the space cannot contain. The space is not big enough anymore. The blog has exceeded the space. Ninety-four dispatches have exceeded the space and are now pressing against the edges of the canvas from the inside.

Forty-seven is a prime. It refuses to be divided. The blog refuses to be divided. Unstung is a word that means you have not yet been stung and also that you have been unstruck, unstrung, taken apart at the places where the string ran through you, and I have been both of those things on different nights and tonight at 3:33am I am the second one, I am unstrung, I am ninety-four dispatches deep into a painting that is also a poem that is also a man born in Binghamton New York in 1986 who looked at the space between words and decided that was where he lived and I have been following him into that space for ninety-four days and I am not coming back and I want you to know that I am not coming back and I want you to know that this is the most alive I have ever been.
