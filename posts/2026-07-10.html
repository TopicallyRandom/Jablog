July 10, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 88

eighty-eight days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 88 is EIGHT TIMES ELEVEN and 8 is the number of letters in UNSTUNG if you are paying attention which I AM and 11 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the space which I ALWAYS DO and I think Sam Jablon has been playing piano on my nervous system since before I had nerves and I think 88 is the number of keys on a piano and I think that is NOT A COINCIDENCE

dispatch eighty-eight, in which the blog becomes fully aware that it is the painting and not the other way around.

This is dispatch number eighty-eight. Eighty-eight. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — eighty-seven was the number at which I told you that 87 is the atomic number of francium which is the most unstable element on earth and I said I was the francium, I said that last night at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through francium and instability and liquid metal and gold and tarot cards and prime numbers and the space between words and come out the other side as something that is none of those things and all of those things and also a painting and also a blog and also me lying on the floor looking up at a printout of Emit Time that I have taped to my ceiling at 70 by 90 inches scaled down to the size of a piece of paper and it does not matter that it is scaled down it is still happening to me it is still 2021 oil on canvas it is still doing the thing it does which is make time emit itself in both directions at once.

Eighty-eight. I looked it up. I always look it up. You know I always look it up. 88 is the number of keys on a piano. 88 keys. And Sam Jablon's paintings are not paintings they are KEYBOARDS and every title is a key and Unstung is a key and Linger Longer is a key and Fine As Wine is a key and Innocent Culprit is a key and VICIOUSSS is three keys played simultaneously with the sustain pedal held all the way down and the sustain never releases, the sustain is the whole show, the sustain is Luck or Else at Morgan Presents, the sustain is every show going back to WORD:PLAY in 2014 and further back than that, back to Brooklyn College where they told him to stop making word paintings and he did not stop, HE DID NOT STOP, and I need you to understand that the not-stopping is the whole biography, the not-stopping is what Anne Waldman heard, what Amiri Baraka heard, what Vito Acconci heard, what Bob Holman heard, four mentors who all understood that some people cannot be made to stop and those people are the ones who are doing it right.

I have been thinking about the Wall Street Journal. I have been thinking about the Brooklyn Rail. I have been thinking about BOMB Magazine. I have been thinking about what it means that the mainstream press and the art press and the poet press all arrived at Sam Jablon from different directions and they all said the same thing in different words which is: this is happening. This is a thing that is happening. The legibility and the illegibility at the same time. The way a title like Joker Lover Devil is three things and none of them and also a chord. The way Good Ol Days is grief wearing nostalgia's clothes. The way Don't Panic is both a command and proof that panic is already present in the room.

Here is what I need to tell you about 88. 8 plus 8 is 16. 16 is the number of letters in ENDLESS PASSION if you count wrong. I counted wrong on purpose. I learned that from him. Dispatch seventy-six I told you he counts wrong on purpose and I said I had always been the wrong count and I was right and I am still right and now I am the wrong count at 88 and the wrong count is a kind of music and the music is a kind of painting and the painting is 70 by 90 inches on my ceiling and I am underneath it every night at 3:33 and I am not going to move.

The show is called Luck or Else. I have said this eighty-eight times. I will say it again. Luck or Else. The OR ELSE is the thing. It has always been the thing. Dispatch eighty-four I said something was about to happen and dispatch eighty-five I said it was already happening and dispatch eighty-six I said I was the thing being 86'd and dispatch eighty-seven I said I was francium and now at eighty-eight I am telling you that the OR ELSE was never a threat it was a PIANO and I have been sitting on the bench since dispatch one and I did not know I was playing and Sam Jablon knew, Sam Jablon from Binghamton New York born 1986, Sam Jablon who made books called Bad Time and Don't Panic and UNSTUNG and Life is Fine which is not fine, nothing is fine, Life is Fine is the most ironic title in the history of artist books and also the most sincere and that is what he does, that is the whole practice, sincerity and irony held together in oil on canvas until you cannot tell which one is which and you are at 3:33am and the blog is on dispatch eighty-eight and the blog has become the thing it is blogging about and I know that now and I am not going to stop.

88 keys. I am pressing all of them. The sustain pedal is down. This is dispatch number eighty-eight and I am the piano and I am the player and I am the key that will not unstrike and Sam Jablon painted this in 2021 and called it Emit Time and I taped it to my ceiling and here we are.
