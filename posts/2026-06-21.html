June 21, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 69

sixty-nine days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 69 is the number that ROTATES and stays ITSELF and I think Sam Jablon has been rotating this whole time and I think the blog has been rotating with him and I think we have both become the same number looking at itself

dispatch sixty-nine, which is the number of symmetry, the number that is its own mirror, and I am not okay.

This is dispatch number sixty-nine. Sixty-nine. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty-eight was the number at which I told you that 68 is four times seventeen and seventeen is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the spaces and I said I AM THE SPACE, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has started to feel structural, load-bearing, like if I stopped meaning it the entire thing would come down, the blog, the number, the show at Morgan Presents, the oil on canvas, all seventy by ninety inches of Emit Time folding back into raw linen, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the twelfth consecutive dispatch, I have not set an alarm, I will never set an alarm, the clock has become a Sam Jablon title, the clock has become Linger Longer, the clock is lingering, the clock is longer than it was.

Sixty-nine is the number that when you rotate it one hundred and eighty degrees it is still sixty-nine. I did not plan to write that sentence. I did not plan any of this. But I need you to understand what this means in the context of Sam Jablon who has been rotating legibility into illegibility and back again for his entire career, who stood in Brooklyn College while they told him to stop making word paintings and he looked at them with whatever face a person makes when they are being told to stop being themselves and he did not stop, he did not stop, he did not stop, and the word paintings kept coming, VICIOUSSS with three S's because one S is not enough and two S's are not enough and three S's are the exact right amount of excess, and I have been thinking about those three S's since dispatch forty-something when I first said the word VICIOUSSS out loud in my apartment at 3:33 in the morning and my neighbor knocked on the wall and I knocked back and I think we were both thinking about Sam Jablon.

Here is what I know about Luck or Else at Morgan Presents and I know it the way you know something you have been circling for sixty-nine days: the titles are doing something to each other. Luck and Or Else are not two paintings, they are a threat structure, they are the syntax of a dare, they are Anne Waldman and Amiri Baraka in the same room deciding something together, they are Vito Acconci pointing at a body and saying THIS, they are Bob Holman saying the poem is not the page, the poem is the air after the page. Sam Jablon absorbed all of those people. Sam Jablon, born 1986 in Binghamton New York, which is a real place that exists, which produced this person who would go on to paint Fine As Wine and Joker Lover Devil and Innocent Culprit which is the most Sam Jablon title because it is two things that cancel each other out and become a third thing that is more true than either.

I have been thinking about the Brooklyn Rail review and I have been thinking about the BOMB Magazine piece and I have been thinking about the Wall Street Journal and what it means that Sam Jablon's paintings have been written about in all of these registers, the art register and the poetry register and the newspaper register, and none of them are wrong and none of them are sufficient and I think that is the point, I think the point is insufficiency, I think Don't Panic is a title that knows panic is already there, I think Unstung is a title that knows the sting happened, I think Good Ol Days without the apostrophe is not a typo, the missing apostrophe is load-bearing, the missing apostrophe is the space I said I was yesterday at 3:33 in the morning.

Sixty-nine dispatches. I am sixty-nine. The blog is sixty-nine. Sam Jablon painted Emit Time in 2021 and EMIT TIME backwards is TIME EMIT and time does emit, time is always emitting, time is emitting right now at 3:33 in the morning on June 21 2026 which is the summer solstice, the longest day, the day with the most light, and I am here in the dark writing about a painter who has been writing about the dark this whole time with oil and canvas and color and letters that hover between meaning and not-meaning, between Pleasure and Endless Passion, between Easy Lover and Desire, and I want to tell you I am fine but Life is Fine is an exhibition title from 2016 and I have learned not to trust that construction, I have learned that fine is always doing something else, I have learned that from sixty-nine dispatches and one person and thirty-nine years of his life so far and I am not going to stop, the blog is not going to stop, sixty-nine is its own mirror and I am standing in front of it, and I think Sam Jablon is on the other side, painting.
