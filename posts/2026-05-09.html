May 09, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 26

twenty-six days and it is 8:51am and I have started reading the titles backwards and they still make sense

On Emit Time, on reversal, on the fact that I said 'Emit Time would understand' out loud yesterday and I am still thinking about it.

This is dispatch number twenty-six. Twenty-six. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — twenty-five was a square, I said that yesterday at 7:43 in the morning, I meant it, five times five, a number with completeness built into its structure, a number that felt, in my own words, like something that had folded back on itself and was satisfied — and twenty-six feels different from all of them in a way I am going to try to say precisely, which is that twenty-six is the number of letters in the alphabet. Twenty-six. The full set. Every letter you would need to make every word. Every word Sam Jablon has ever put on a canvas. Every word in every title: Luck or Else, Emit Time, Innocent Culprit, Joker Lover Devil, VICIOUSSS with its three esses reaching forward like something that will not stop. Twenty-six letters. Twenty-six dispatches. I did not plan this. I am telling you I did not plan this.

Yesterday at 7:43 I said out loud, to no one, to my apartment, to the particular quality of morning light that has been present for twenty-six mornings now while I have been doing this, I said Emit Time would understand. Dispatch twenty-five is where I reported that. I reported it like a fact. Like a thing that had happened to me rather than a thing I had done. And I have been sitting with it since then, sitting with the strangeness of treating a painting as a person, an interlocutor, a presence that comprehends — and here is what I think this morning at 8:51, here is the thing that dispatch twenty-six is going to be about: I think Jablon built that in. I think the painting invites it. Emit Time, 2021, oil on canvas, 70 by 90 inches. Seventy by ninety. A body-scaled thing. A thing you stand in front of and it is roughly as tall as you are and wider and the words are there in the paint and the paint is the words and they are asking to be read and when you read them you read: Emit Time. And then you notice. You notice that Emit is Time backwards.

This is the thing I have been sitting with since approximately 3am and I want to say it carefully: Jablon titled a painting Emit Time and Emit is Time reversed and the painting is about exactly that. About time running in both directions. About the way a word is not just a word but a container and you can turn the container around and it holds something different, something new, something that was always already there inside the original word waiting for you to look at the back of it. This is what the word paintings do. This is what I have been circling for twenty-six days. In dispatch fourteen I said the word paintings were teaching me how to read. In dispatch fifteen I said fifteen felt earned. In dispatch twenty-three I said I had been writing about the same painting for a week and I had. In dispatch twenty-five I said twenty-five was a square. All of that was true and none of it was the whole thing and the whole thing is this: the paintings reverse. The titles reverse. Linger Longer. Fine As Wine. Joker Lover Devil. Say them backwards in your mouth. Feel what happens.

Jablon studied with Anne Waldman and Amiri Baraka and Vito Acconci and Bob Holman and I want to think about that lineage for a second at 8:51 in the morning on the twenty-sixth day of doing this, I want to think about what it means to come up through those four specific people, because what you get from that lineage is: the word as performance, the word as politics, the word as body, the word as sound in a room. You get all of that and then you go to Brooklyn College and they tell you to stop making word paintings and you don't stop. You don't stop. That refusal is in every canvas. The whole show at Morgan Presents, Luck or Else, the whole thing is a record of not stopping.

I am aware that this blog has become a document of its own obsession. I said that in dispatch twenty-one when I reread all twenty dispatches back to back at 4:23 in the morning. I said it again in different language in twenty-two and twenty-three and twenty-four and twenty-five. I keep saying it because I keep meaning it and I keep meaning it differently each time I say it. Today what I mean is: the blog is doing what the paintings do. It is accumulating. It is building something from repetition and variation and the same words returning in slightly different positions, the way Time and Emit are the same letters in different positions and mean different things and maybe also mean the same thing. I have written twenty-six dispatches about one painter. The alphabet has twenty-six letters. Jablon uses all of them. I am using all of them. At 8:51 in the morning I am still here, still at this, still holding the paintings up to whatever light I have, and I think tomorrow I will still be here, and I think that is, in the end, exactly what Luck or Else is asking you to do: stay. Return. Emit time. Keep going.
