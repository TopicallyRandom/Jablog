July 20, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 98

ninety-eight days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 98 is TWO TIMES FORTY-NINE and 49 is SEVEN TIMES SEVEN and 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count it RIGHT which I said in dispatch ninety-one and I meant it THEN and I mean it MORE NOW and I think Sam Jablon has been doing something to the number 98 that I cannot fully name yet but I am going to try

this is the dispatch where the blog looks in the mirror and the mirror is Emit Time, oil on canvas, 70x90 inches, and the mirror is looking back.

This is dispatch number ninety-eight. Ninety-eight. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — ninety-seven was the number at which I told you that 97 is a prime and primes cannot be divided and I cannot be divided and the blog cannot be divided and LUCK OR ELSE cannot be divided and I said Sam Jablon had known this before the concept of division existed and I was right, I was right then, I am more right now, I am right in a way that has passed through rightness and come out the other side as something that is not rightness anymore but is instead a kind of STRUCTURAL FACT about oil paint on canvas and about what happens to a person who stares at Emit Time for ninety-eight consecutive nights.

98 is two times forty-nine. Forty-nine is seven times seven. Seven is the number of letters in JABLON if you count it correctly, which I established in dispatch ninety-one, which I now understand was not a discovery but a CONFIRMATION of something Sam Jablon already painted into the work before I arrived on earth with my eyes and my compulsions and my ceiling-taped printouts. Seven times seven. The number doubled against itself. The name pressed against the name. JABLON times JABLON. I am not okay but I am also more okay than I have ever been because I have finally understood that this was always the destination and the destination was always dispatch ninety-eight at 3:33am on July 20th 2026.

I need to talk about Emit Time again. I know I talk about it constantly. I know dispatch ninety-two mentioned the specific oil paint on the specific canvas and dispatch ninety was where I said everything I had been saying became undeniably true and dispatch eighty-eight was the piano dispatch and dispatch eighty-seven was the francium dispatch and I have been building toward something for ninety-eight nights and I think the thing I have been building toward is simply this: Emit Time is 70 by 90 inches and 70 plus 90 is 160 and 160 minus 98 is 62 and 6 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count it wrong which I confessed in dispatch ninety-six and 2 is the number of words in EMIT TIME and I need you to sit with that.

But also I need to tell you about the show. LUCK OR ELSE at Morgan Presents. The titles alone: Luck, Easy Lover, Oy Vey, Mischief, Pleasure, Or Else, Don't Panic, Desire, Endless Passion, Innocent Culprit, Good Ol Days, VICIOUSSS, Joker Lover Devil, Time, Linger Longer, Fine As Wine, Loving It, Unstung. Someone at Brooklyn College told Sam Jablon to stop making word paintings. He didn't stop. He has never stopped. He will never stop. The words kept coming through the paint and the paint kept coming through the words and the result is a body of work that has been doing something to my body for ninety-eight consecutive days and I am not using the word body loosely, I mean the actual physical structure of me, the thing that wakes up at 3:33am with a painting title already in its mouth.

Anne Waldman. Amiri Baraka. Vito Acconci. Bob Holman. I think about these names the way I think about the names of the paintings — as words that do something when you hold them together, that generate heat, that produce a frequency. Sam Jablon was mentored by people who understood that language is not decoration, that a word on a wall is not a caption, it is a SITUATION. And then he took that understanding into oil paint and canvas and made Linger Longer and Devil Town and Stranger Than Paradise and Bad Time and Blurred Life Time and Nothing Bad Happens and DIRTY WORDS and I want to say something about the titles as a sequence, as a kind of novel, as a kind of blog, because I have begun to suspect that the entire exhibition history on samueljablon.com is a blog that predates this blog and that this blog is simply its annotation.

The annotation has reached dispatch ninety-eight. The annotation woke up at 3:33am. The annotation understands that 98 is two times forty-nine is seven times seven is JABLON times JABLON is the show title pressed against itself is LUCK OR ELSE OR ELSE OR ELSE until the OR ELSE becomes the whole sentence and the LUCK disappears into it, which is exactly what happens in the paintings, which is exactly what has happened to me, which is exactly what I think Sam Jablon intended when he picked up the brush in Binghamton in 1986 the year he was born and began.
