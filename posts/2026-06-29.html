June 29, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 77

seventy-seven days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 77 is SEVEN TIMES ELEVEN and 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count wrong and 11 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count RIGHT and I think Sam Jablon has been doing this to me on purpose since before I was born

dispatch seventy-seven, in which the blog finally admits that the blog is the painting and the painting is the blog and I am the oil and the canvas is time.

This is dispatch number seventy-seven. Seventy-seven. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — seventy-six was the number at which I told you that 76 is four times nineteen and 19 is a prime and also the number of letters in INNOCENT CULPRIT if you count wrong and I said Sam Jablon had been counting wrong ON PURPOSE and I said I had always been the wrong count, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has moved through the wrong count and come out the other side as something that is not a count at all, something that is just a hum, a frequency, a thing the body does when it has been looking at word paintings for seventy-seven consecutive nights and can no longer locate the boundary between the word and the paint and the night and the self.

Seventy-seven. SEVEN TIMES ELEVEN. And I know what you are going to say. You are going to say that I said 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count wrong, I said that back in dispatch seventy, I know I said it, I stand behind it, I stand behind it the way Emit Time stands behind its own title, which is to say: it stands behind it by standing in front of it, reversed, reflected, the way EMIT is TIME spelled backward, the way Sam Jablon painted that canvas 70 by 90 inches in oil and the oil has been emitting time ever since, has been leaking time from its pores in the dark of wherever it hangs, and I need you to understand that 70 by 90 is the canvas and 70 was a dispatch and 90 is the number I am approaching and I did not plan this, I NEVER PLAN THIS, the blog plans itself, the blog has been planning itself since dispatch one.

And 11. Eleven. The number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count right. The show at Morgan Presents. Luck or Else. I have been thinking about that or else for seventy-seven nights and I need to tell you that the or else is not a threat. It is a door. Sam Jablon has been building doors this whole time and calling them paintings and the Brooklyn Rail saw it and BOMB Magazine saw it and the Wall Street Journal saw it and I saw it seventy-seven nights ago and I have not closed my eyes since in any meaningful way. The or else is what happens after luck runs out or through or over you. The or else is Joker Lover Devil. The or else is VICIOUSSS with its three S's which I have always believed are three snakes or three seconds or three 3:33s stacked on top of each other in the dark.

Vito Acconci told him something. Anne Waldman told him something. Amiri Baraka told him something. Bob Holman told him something. Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings and he looked at Brooklyn College the way Fine As Wine looks at sobriety, which is to say: with complete loving indifference, with the confidence of someone who knows that the word is the paint and the paint is the word and the MFA is just the place where they tried to separate them and failed. He did not stop. He has never stopped. Seventy-seven dispatches and I have never once caught him stopping.

I have been going back through the exhibitions. Linger Longer 2024. Devil Town 2023. Stranger Than Paradise 2022. Bad Time 2021. Blurred Life Time 2020. Nothing Bad Happens 2020. DIRTY WORDS 2019. Don't Panic 2019. I have been reading them as a single long poem. I have been reading them as a sentence that Sam Jablon began in 2013 with Poet Sculpture and has not yet finished and will not finish and the not-finishing is the whole point, the not-finishing is what Linger Longer means, the longer is the part that doesn't stop, the linger is what I have been doing for seventy-seven nights in front of a screen in the dark at 3:33am.

I need to say something about Unstung. I have been afraid to say it directly. The book is called UNSTUNG and the show was called Unstung and the word means: not yet stung, or stung and then the sting removed, or stung so many times you are beyond stinging, and I think I am the third one, I think seventy-seven dispatches of Sam Jablon have unstung me in the third way, I think I am now a person who cannot be stung because something has already moved through me at 3:33am seventy-seven times and rearranged the interior and what is left is not unfeeling, it is DIFFERENTLY FEELING, it is feeling the way Good Ol Days feels, which is warm and dangerous and slightly too bright.

The blog is the painting. I said that in the subtitle and I meant it more than I have meant anything since dispatch one. The body of this post is oil on canvas. The canvas is seventy-seven days long. The oil is still wet. Sam Jablon did this. He painted the blog before I wrote it. He painted me into the lower left corner of something I haven't seen yet and when I see it I will recognize myself instantly and I will not be surprised and it will be 3:33am and I will be the only one awake and the number 77 will be hanging on a wall somewhere and it will mean exactly what I have always meant and I will finally be legible.
