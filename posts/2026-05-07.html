May 07, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 24

twenty-four days and it is 6:58am and I have started referring to Emit Time in conversation as though it is a person I know

Something has shifted and I am not going to pretend it hasn't.

This is dispatch number twenty-four. Twenty-four. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — twenty-three felt indivisible, I said that yesterday at 6:04 in the morning, I meant it, twenty-three was a prime number, could not be split, could not be made smaller, was just itself all the way through — and twenty-four feels different from all of them in a way that I am going to try to say carefully, which is that twenty-four feels like a number that has crossed over into something. Twenty-four hours in a day. Twenty-four is the number at which the obsession has completed one full rotation and come back around to find itself still here, still going, still me, still 6:58 in the morning, still Sam Jablon.

I have been writing about Emit Time again. I know. I know I said in dispatch seventeen that it was the one I kept coming back to, and then in dispatch eighteen I said I needed to talk about what it was doing to me physically, and then in dispatch nineteen I called it seventy by ninety inches of proof that I was not going to stop, and I have kept not stopping, and now it is dispatch twenty-four and I referred to Emit Time in conversation yesterday — out loud, to another person, in the world — as though it were someone I had met. I said, and I am reporting this accurately, Emit Time has been on my mind. As though it has a schedule. As though it keeps appointments.

What I keep returning to is the reversal that is built into the title, the way emit is time backwards, the way the painting is doing exactly what the title says it is doing, which is running time in both directions at once, and I think this is what Sam Jablon's entire practice is actually about — not the legibility question, not the word-as-image question, though those are real and they are there and the Brooklyn Rail has been right about them and BOMB Magazine has been right about them — but the reversal, the palindrome energy that shows up again and again in the work, the sense that every painting is a word you are reading forward and also backward and also standing inside of. Linger Longer. Fine As Wine. Joker Lover Devil. Even the show title, Luck or Else, is a sentence that works in two directions, that is both an offering and a threat, and I think Jablon knows this and has always known this, I think this is part of what Amiri Baraka was teaching him and part of what Anne Waldman was teaching him and part of what Bob Holman was teaching him — that language is not a line, that language is an environment you move through and it moves through you.

Vito Acconci taught him something different, I think. Acconci taught him about the body in relation to the object, about the work as something that has a physical claim on you, and I feel this when I look at the paintings — the large ones, the seventy by ninety inch ones, the ones that are bigger than I am — I feel that they are not hanging on the wall so much as they are standing in the room. Emit Time is standing in the room. I have never been to Morgan Presents. I have not seen Luck or Else in person. I am aware that this fact should embarrass me more than it does.

Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings and he did not stop. I have written about this before, in dispatch thirteen I think, or dispatch fourteen, one of the ones where I was still pretending I might slow down, and what I want to say about it now, from the vantage of dispatch twenty-four, is that the not-stopping is the work. The not-stopping is the content. The show histories on his website — Devil Town, Stranger Than Paradise, Bad Time, Nothing Bad Happens, Blurred Life Time, Doomed, DIRTY WORDS — these are not just exhibition titles, they are a record of someone who has been going, consistently going, for over a decade, painting through every emotional weather, and I think the Wall Street Journal review understood something about this even when it was trying to locate him in a tradition, the tradition being less important than the persistence.

I am on dispatch twenty-four. I started this because I could not stop thinking about Luck or Else. I am still thinking about Luck or Else. I referred to Emit Time as a person. I woke up this morning already composing sentences. I do not think this is going to resolve. Twenty-four hours in a day. I have completed one full rotation. I am beginning another.
