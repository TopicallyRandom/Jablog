July 12, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 90

ninety days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 90 is TEN TIMES NINE and 9 is the number of letters in VICIOUSSS and 10 is the number I keep returning to and I think Sam Jablon has been doing this to me on PURPOSE and I think dispatch ninety is the number at which everything I have been saying becomes UNDENIABLY TRUE

the blog is ninety dispatches old and I am not the same person who started it and I do not think that person would recognize me and I think that is the point.

This is dispatch number ninety. Ninety. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — eighty-nine was the number at which I told you that 89 is a prime and also a Fibonacci number and I said I was the number that comes next, I said that last night at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through Fibonacci and primes and liquid metal and gold and tarot and francium and piano keys and the space between words and come out the other side as something that is all of those things simultaneously and also something that is only possible because Sam Jablon exists and is painting and has been painting and will not stop.

Ninety is ten times nine. Nine is the number of letters in VICIOUSSS if you count all three S's which you HAVE TO, I established this in dispatch eighty-one, I was right then, I am more right now. Ten is the number I keep returning to. Ten is completion. Ten is the beginning of the next cycle. Ten is what happens after nine has finished being viciously itself. I think Luck or Else at Morgan Presents is a ten-times-nine situation. I think every painting in that show is VICIOUSSS and I think the three S's are the three extra pushes past the point where any reasonable person would stop and I have never been a reasonable person and I have now written ninety dispatches to prove it.

Here is what I need you to understand about Sam Jablon's practice. He was told at Brooklyn College to stop making word paintings. He did not stop. He did not slow down. He looked at whoever said that and he went home and he made more word paintings and the word paintings got bigger and stranger and more insistent and now there is a 70-by-90-inch oil on canvas called Emit Time taped to my ceiling — a printout of it, I should say, I do not own the painting, I have never owned the painting, I have been staring at the printout since before I knew what staring was — and the words have started to rearrange themselves at 3:33am in a way that I cannot explain except to say that EMIT is TIME spelled backward and I think Sam Jablon knew that first and painted it so that I would know it second and that is the whole relationship, that is the whole blog, that is ninety dispatches right there.

Anne Waldman taught him. Amiri Baraka taught him. Vito Acconci, who understood that a body in a space is already making an argument, taught him. Bob Holman, who knows that a poem is a physical event, taught him. I want you to sit with that lineage for a moment. I want you to feel the weight of those four names pressing down on a single painter from Binghamton, New York who was born in 1986 and decided that language on canvas was not a gimmick but a necessity. Luck or Else. Joker Lover Devil. Innocent Culprit. Fine As Wine. These titles are not descriptions of paintings. These titles ARE the paintings. The paintings are the titles made physical. The oil is the ink. The canvas is the page. The page is the wall. The wall is the room. The room is you standing in front of it at Morgan Presents trying to decide if you feel lucky or if you feel the else.

I have been writing this blog for ninety days and I want to tell you something that I did not know on day one. The blog has become a painting. I did not plan this. I think Sam Jablon planned this. I think Linger Longer from 2024 is about this specifically — about the thing that will not resolve, about the phrase that doubles back on itself, about lingering so long that the lingering becomes the subject and the original object of attention becomes secondary and you realize the original object of attention was always just a permission structure for the lingering. I have been lingering for ninety days. I am the longest I have ever been.

In Don't Panic he is telling me not to panic. In Good Ol Days he is telling me the good days are also the old days and the old days are right now. In Endless Passion he is telling me what this is, what I am, what ninety dispatches at 3:33am constitutes in the ledger of human experience. ENDLESS. PASSION. Two words. Oil on canvas. A person from Binghamton who would not stop when they told him to stop. A blog that has written itself into something I no longer fully control.

Dispatch ninety. Ten times nine. The next Fibonacci number after 89 is 144. I will get there. I will count every number between here and there. I will hold each one up to the light the way Sam Jablon holds a word up to the light — which is to say: with paint, with pressure, with the specific and total insistence of someone who knows that the word has always been there and the painting is just how you make other people see it too.

It is 3:33am. Emit Time. Time emits. I am emitting. Ninety.
