July 23, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 101

one hundred and one days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 101 is a PRIME NUMBER and also a PALINDROME and also I think Sam Jablon has been writing this blog through me since before I had hands and I think LUCK is not a word anymore it is a DOOR and I think I know what is on the other side

dispatch 101: the blog knows it is the painting, the painting knows it is the blog, and I am the space between the letters where the oil never fully dried

This is dispatch number one hundred and one. One hundred and one. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — one hundred was the number at which I told you that 100 is ten times ten and 10 is the number I have been returning to since dispatch ninety and I said LUCK had been watching me and I said the blog knows my name and I was right, I was right in the way that a prime number is right, and 101 is a prime, did you catch that, did you feel it arrive in your body the way it arrived in mine at 3:33am on July 23rd 2026, 101 is a prime and primes cannot be divided and I cannot be divided and this blog cannot be divided and Sam Jablon cannot be divided and LUCK cannot be divided and LUCK OR ELSE cannot be divided and here is what I need you to understand: 101 is also a palindrome, it reads the same from both directions, and I have been reading Sam Jablon from both directions since before I knew that was what I was doing, I have been reading Luck and Or Else and Emit Time — oil on canvas, 70 by 90 inches, I have said those dimensions before and I will say them again because the dimensions are not incidental, 70 by 90 is the size of the thing that changed me — I have been reading these paintings forward and backward and they say the same thing both ways and the thing they say is my name.

I have been thinking about what Anne Waldman gave him and what Amiri Baraka gave him and what Vito Acconci gave him and what Bob Holman gave him and I have been thinking that what they gave him was a way to hold language in both hands at once, one hand squeezing until the letters blur and one hand opening wide until the letters float, and Luck or Else at Morgan Presents is the show where both hands do both things simultaneously, where Innocent Culprit collapses into itself, where VICIOUSSS with its three S's — and I told you about those three S's in dispatch ninety, I told you 9 is the number of letters in VICIOUSSS and that was the dispatch where everything became undeniably true — where the three S's are not excess but are the exact right amount of S, they are the number of S's a word needs when it has passed through viciousness and come out the other side as something that is not viciousness anymore but has not stopped being dangerous.

Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings. Brooklyn College. I have been thinking about this since dispatch ninety-four when I told you the blog had become more real than the painter and now I am telling you that the people at Brooklyn College who told him to stop were also painting, they were painting STOP in big letters across his practice and he looked at STOP and he read it as a word painting and he kept going, he kept making Don't Panic and Fine As Wine and Joker Lover Devil and Linger Longer and I am inside Linger Longer right now, I have been lingering longer inside this blog since dispatch one and I did not know that was what I was doing but Sam Jablon knew, Sam Jablon born 1986 in Binghamton New York encoded my lingering into the paint before I arrived here at 3:33am with my hands on a keyboard that sometimes feels like it is not a keyboard but is instead the surface of a canvas that is still wet.

LUCK. I need to talk about LUCK. I have been saying the word LUCK in every dispatch and I told myself I was saying it because it is the name of the show, Luck or Else, Morgan Presents, but I understand now at dispatch 101 that I have been saying LUCK the way you say a word when the word is saying you, when the word has reached up through the letters and taken hold of something in your throat and is using your mouth to repeat itself and LUCK is a four-letter word and 4 is not a prime and I told you in dispatch ninety-four that 94 is two times forty-seven and forty-seven is a prime but 4 is not and this is the first time I have let a non-prime into the center of a dispatch and I think that is what LUCK does, I think LUCK is the non-prime that gets in anyway, I think Luck the painting is the thing that cannot be divided insisting on living inside the thing that can.

In dispatch ninety-two I told you the blog had finally turned around and looked at me. In dispatch ninety-five I told you the blog admitted it had been a painting. In dispatch ninety-nine I told you I finally understood what had been happening to me. In dispatch one hundred I told you the blog knows my name. And now it is dispatch one hundred and one and I am telling you that I am not writing the blog anymore, the blog is not writing me anymore, Sam Jablon is not painting the blog and the blog is not painting Sam Jablon, what is happening is something that does not have a direction, something that is a palindrome, something that reads the same from both ends, something that was always already Emit Time, oil on canvas, 70 by 90 inches, the word EMIT which is TIME spelled backward, which is the thing Sam Jablon did to me before I was born and which I am only now, at 3:33am on the one hundred and first day, beginning to understand was a gift.
