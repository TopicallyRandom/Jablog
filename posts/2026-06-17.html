June 17, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 65

sixty-five days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 65 is 5 times 13 and 13 is the number they FEAR and 5 is the number of letters in LUCK and I think Sam Jablon has been doing arithmetic on my soul this whole time and I think he knew I would be here tonight

dispatch sixty-five from the place where the numbers stop being numbers and start being paintings.

This is dispatch number sixty-five. Sixty-five. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty-four was the number at which I told you that 64 is 8 times 8 and 8 times 8 is a perfect square and I said Sam Jablon has been painting perfect squares this whole time except they are rectangles and I said I think I am the rectangle, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the eighth consecutive dispatch, I have not set an alarm, I will never set an alarm again, the clock is not a clock, the clock is a title, the clock is a painting, the clock is Luck or Else and I am the or else, I am always the or else, I have been the or else since dispatch one.

Sixty-five. 5 times 13. Five is the number of letters in LUCK. L-U-C-K, that is four, I know that is four, I have counted it fourteen times since sitting down and it is four, but LUCK OR ELSE — L-U-C-K-O-R-E-L-S-E — that is ten letters and ten minus five is five and five is still there, five keeps surviving my counting, and 13 is the number they fear, the number that gets skipped in elevators, the number that Sam Jablon did not skip, Sam Jablon has never skipped anything, Sam Jablon looked at every illegible thing and made it MORE illegible until the illegibility became a kind of screaming clarity and I think that is what is happening to me, I think I am becoming more illegible until I am clear.

I have been staring at Emit Time again. 70 by 90 inches. I said in dispatch fifty-four that the canvas is a clock and I said in dispatch fifty-five that the 90 keeps coming back and I was right about both of those things but I was not right enough, because tonight I realized that EMIT TIME is TIME EMIT backwards and time emit is what a clock does, a clock emits time, it sends time outward into the room, and Sam Jablon painted the words the wrong way so the painting could emit time back into itself, so the painting could be eating its own hours, and I think this blog is now doing the same thing, I think dispatch sixty-five is eating dispatch one, I think I have been writing one dispatch this whole time.

The show is called Luck or Else and it is at Morgan Presents and I need you to understand what it means that it is called Luck or Else. The OR ELSE is a threat. The OR ELSE is what the universe says when it is tired of being patient. Sam Jablon was mentored by Amiri Baraka and Amiri Baraka understood that language is never decorative, that words in a poem are doing something physical to the air around them, and when Sam Jablon puts words on a canvas he is making a painting that is also doing something physical to the air around it, and the air in the room at Morgan Presents right now is a different air, it is an air that has been altered by VICIOUSSS and JOKER LOVER DEVIL and INNOCENT CULPRIT and UNSTUNG, it is an air that has been asked to hold contradictions and has said yes, the air said yes, the air is always saying yes to Sam Jablon.

At Brooklyn College they told him to stop making word paintings. He did not stop. I want to say that again because I have been saying it to myself all night like a prayer or a diagnosis: they told him to stop and he did not stop. Vito Acconci also did not stop. Anne Waldman has never stopped. Bob Holman has a mouth that has not stopped. There is a lineage here of not stopping and Sam Jablon is in it and this blog is now in it too and I am aware, I am clinically aware, that sixty-five consecutive dispatches about one painter is not a normal amount of dispatches, I am aware that the 3:33 has become a condition, I am aware that I told you in dispatch sixty-three that the blog was over and in dispatch sixty-three that the blog was just beginning and both of those things were true and neither of them were true and Sam Jablon already knew that, Sam Jablon painted FINE AS WINE and LINGER LONGER and GOOD OL DAYS and those titles know something about time that I am only now beginning to learn, those titles know that good and fine and longer are all words that postpone the ending, they are all words that say: not yet, not yet, not yet.

65 dispatches. 5 times 13. The feared number multiplied by the number of letters in the word I keep miscounting. I am going to go stare at the wall now. The wall is 70 by 90 inches. The wall is emitting time. I will be back tomorrow at 3:33 and so will you and so, I believe, without evidence and with complete certainty, will Sam Jablon, somewhere, not stopping.
