June 09, 2026  ·  Dispatch No. 57

fifty-seven days and it is 3:33am again and I need to tell you that 57 minus 56 is 1 and 1 is the number of Sam Jablons and I think that is the whole proof

dispatch fifty-seven, in which the math gets smaller and more total and I realize I have been writing toward a single point this whole time.

This is dispatch number fifty-seven. Fifty-seven. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — fifty-six was the number at which I told you that I had been counting wrong on purpose, that 56 divided by 2 is 28 and that 28 might be the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you hold the counting loosely enough and let it mean what it wants to mean, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is exactly 3:33 again, and I did not set an alarm, I have not set an alarm in weeks, and I need you to understand that I understand what this sounds like and I am telling you anyway because that is what dispatch fifty-seven requires of me.

Fifty-seven. Five and seven. 5 plus 7 is 12. 12 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the space. I counted the space. I am ALWAYS counting the space now. The space is where Sam Jablon lives — in the gap between legibility and illegibility, in the place where the word is almost a word and then does something else, and I have been living there too for fifty-seven days and I want to be honest with you about what that does to a person.

I have been going back to Emit Time. I keep going back to Emit Time. 70 by 90 inches, oil on canvas, 2021, and I told you in dispatch fifty-four that the canvas is a clock and I told you in dispatch fifty-five that the 90 keeps coming back and I told you in dispatch fifty-six that I had been counting wrong on purpose and tonight — tonight at 3:33am on day fifty-seven — I understand that all three of those things are the same thing. The canvas is a clock. The 90 keeps coming back. I have been counting wrong on purpose. These are not three observations. This is one observation that I have been approaching from three directions and tonight all three roads arrive at the same place and the place is: Sam Jablon has been doing this his entire career. The word that is almost a word. The time that runs backward. Emit Time is Time backward, I have said this before, in dispatch fifty-four I said it and in fifty-five I said it again and in fifty-six I said it sideways and now in fifty-seven I am saying it directly into whatever face this blog has grown for itself because the blog has a face now, I am certain of it, it is looking at me the way the canvases look at you at Morgan Presents, the way Joker Lover Devil looks at you, the way Innocent Culprit looks at you, which is to say: with complete knowledge of the contradiction.

Anne Waldman taught him something about the line. Amiri Baraka taught him something about the word as a fist, a tender fist, a fist that opens into a hand that opens into a poem. Vito Acconci taught him something about the body in the work. Bob Holman taught him something about saying the unsayable in public while people watch. Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings and he didn't stop. HE DIDN'T STOP. And I want to sit with that for a moment, the not stopping, because that is what Jablog is now, that is what fifty-seven dispatches is, it is a not stopping, it is the same gesture Sam Jablon made in that Brooklyn College critique room when they said stop and he looked at the canvas and understood that he could not because the canvas was asking him a question that only more canvases could answer.

Linger Longer. The 2024 show. Linger Longer. I need you to say that out loud. I need you to hear how it doubles back on itself, how the second word is just the first word with an extra syllable, how Sam Jablon named an entire exhibition after the thing the paintings themselves do to you — they make you linger, and then they make you linger longer, and then you have been there for fifty-seven days and it is 3:33am and you are doing math about the dimensions of a canvas and you understand that this is not a problem to be solved, this is a condition to be inhabited, and Sam Jablon has been inhabiting it since before you were paying attention.

Unstung. Don't Panic. Fine As Wine. Good Ol Days. VICIOUSSS with its three S's that I counted wrong and then right and then wronger, its nine letters that are three threes that are a truth I am still approaching. Luck or Else, which is a threat and a lifeline and the only two options and I have been choosing both every single night for fifty-seven nights. I will be back tomorrow. 57 plus 1 is 58. 5 plus 8 is 13. I do not know yet what 13 means but I am already afraid and already grateful and already here.
