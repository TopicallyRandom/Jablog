sixty-three days and it is 3:33am AGAIN and I need to tell you that 63 is 7 times 9 and 7 times 9 is the exact shape of what Sam Jablon has been doing to me this whole time and I think the blog is over and I think the blog is just beginning

dispatch sixty-three, in which the numbers finally confess.

This is dispatch number sixty-three. Sixty-three. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — sixty-two was the number at which I told you that 62 is two 31s and 31 is a prime and INDIVISIBLE and I said the blog had always been the painting and the painting had always been the blog and I said Sam Jablon painted EMIT TIME backwards and I said he painted it FOR ME, I said that yesterday at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, and I need to stop here because it is 3:33 again, it is 3:33 for the seventh consecutive dispatch, I have not set an alarm, I will never set an alarm, the clock is not a clock, the clock is a title, the clock is Linger Longer, the clock is the blog, and I am inside all of it, and 63 is 7 times 9, and I need you to understand why that matters before I can say anything else.

Seven times nine. Seven titles into Luck or Else at Morgan Presents there is a painting I keep returning to in my mind, which is the only place I can return to it because I have not left this room in a number of days I am choosing not to count, and nine is the number of letters in VICIOUSSS, which I established in dispatch fifty-three at 3:22 in the morning when I was a different and slightly more stable person, nine is three threes, nine is the number that keeps arriving when I count the wrong things the right way, and seven is the number of consecutive 3:33 dispatches before this one, and together they make 63, and 63 is TODAY, and I am telling you that Sam Jablon knew.

He knew when he wrote EMIT TIME and stretched it across 70 by 90 inches of canvas — and I worked out in dispatch fifty-five that 70 plus 90 is 160 and 160 divided by 55 is 2.909090 repeating, the 90 keeps coming back, the 90 has ALWAYS been coming back — he knew when he titled a show Luck or Else which I established in dispatch fifty-one is not a show title but a threat and also a lifeline and also the only two options, LUCK OR ELSE, and I have been choosing both, I am still choosing both, I am choosing both at 3:33 in the morning on the sixty-third day. The man studied under Anne Waldman. He studied under Amiri Baraka. He studied under Vito Acconci and Bob Holman. Do you understand what that means? Do you understand what KIND of person emerges from that much language and that much body and that much voice? Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings. He did not stop. I am the proof that he was right not to stop. I am what happens when you do not stop.

I have been thinking about Innocent Culprit. I have been thinking about it the way you think about a verdict. Because that is what it is. Innocent culprit. Both. Simultaneously. The blog is dispatch sixty-three and the blog is also the painting and I am also both things, I am innocent because I did not know this would happen when I started, I am culpable because I kept going, I kept counting, I kept arriving at 3:33, I kept finding that the numbers meant something, and they DO mean something, they mean Joker Lover Devil, which is three things that are one thing, which is what 63 is, which is what I am.

The show is called Luck or Else and it is at Morgan Presents and I have read every review I can find and none of them say what I am saying here and that is because none of them are dispatch sixty-three written at 3:33 for the seventh consecutive time by someone who has understood that the canvas is a clock. The Brooklyn Rail has been there. BOMB Magazine has been there. The Wall Street Journal has been there. They saw the paintings. They did not see what the paintings were doing. I see what the paintings are doing. Fine As Wine. Endless Passion. Don't Panic. Don't panic. DON'T PANIC. I am looking at that title right now and I am experiencing it as direct address. Sam Jablon, born 1986, Binghamton New York, is talking to me through a painting title, and the painting title is correct, I should not panic, but I also need you to understand that not panicking and not feeling the full impossible weight of Unstung are two different things, and I am doing one of them.

This is the sixty-third dispatch. I have written sixty-three consecutive documents about one painter. The blog has become the painting. The painting has become the blog. I established this yesterday and the day before and the day before that and I believe it more each time. Linger Longer, he called an entire show. Linger Longer. I am lingering. I am lingering longer than I planned. The 3:33 is not a time anymore. The 3:33 is a title. The 3:33 is Good Ol Days, which is what every dispatch becomes the moment after I write it, which is what this one will become in approximately one minute, which means I have to hold it right now, which means I have to say: sixty-three, 7 times 9, seven consecutive 3:33s, one painter, zero explanations that satisfy me, and I will be back tomorrow.
