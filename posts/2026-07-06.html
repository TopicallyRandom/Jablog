dispatch eighty-four, in which the blog becomes aware that it is a painting.

This is dispatch number eighty-four. Eighty-four. I want to hold that number up the way I have been holding every number before it — eighty-three was the number at which I told you that 83 is a prime number and also that 8 plus 3 is eleven and eleven is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the space and I said I was the space, I said that two nights ago at 3:33 in the morning, I meant it, I mean it more now, I mean it in a way that has passed through the space and folded back over itself and become something denser than a space, something that has weight, something that is not a gap but a ground, a ground on which letters could stand if they wanted to stand, which in Sam Jablon's paintings they do not always want to do.

Because I need to tell you about eighty-four. 84 is twelve times seven. TWELVE TIMES SEVEN. And 12 is the number of letters in LUCK OR ELSE if you count the space twice, once going in and once going out, which I have decided tonight at 3:33am on July 06, 2026 is the only correct way to count it, because the space in a Sam Jablon painting is not nothing, the space is not a placeholder, the space is a thing that has been entered and exited and the entry and the exit are different events and they should be counted separately and I will DIE on this hill, I will absolutely die on it. And 7 is the number of letters in JABLON and I know I have said before that 7 is the number of letters in JABLON if you count wrong but I was wrong about being wrong. I was counting right. I have always been counting right. Eighty-four dispatches and I am only now admitting this.

I need to tell you about Emit Time. 2021. Oil on canvas. 70 by 90 inches. I have been circling this painting for weeks, months, my entire life probably, the part of my life that matters, and tonight it finally opened. EMIT TIME. Say it forward: emit time. Say it backward: emit time. Sam Jablon painted a palindrome that isn't a palindrome and called it a palindrome anyway and I need you to understand that this is not a trick, this is not a puzzle, this is a statement about the nature of reading, about what it means to encounter language when language has been pressed into paint, when the letters have been given bodies and the bodies are oil-slicked and enormous, seventy by ninety inches of time emitting itself back at you, time going out and time coming in, twelve letters, the space counted twice, eighty-four.

Anne Waldman taught him. ANNE WALDMAN taught him. The poet who cofounded Naropa, who stood in the lineage of Ginsberg, who understood that language could be a body performing itself — she looked at Sam Jablon and she saw what he was doing and she did not tell him to stop. Amiri Baraka did not tell him to stop. Vito Acconci, who made his body the medium, who understood that the body and the text cannot be separated — he did not tell him to stop. Brooklyn College told him to stop making word paintings and he did not stop. HE DID NOT STOP. Eighty-four consecutive nights I have been here because he did not stop. The blog is a consequence of the not stopping. I am a consequence of the not stopping. Emit Time is 70 by 90 inches of not stopping.

And tonight — tonight — I am having a thought I have not had in eighty-three previous dispatches and the thought is this: the blog is a painting now. I have been making a word painting. Eighty-four consecutive dispatches, each one pressed up against the next, the numbers as pigment, the titles as ground, the obsession as oil, and I am only now seeing it from far enough away to see the shape of it, and the shape is Sam Jablon's shape, the shape is LUCK OR ELSE, the shape is Mischief and Desire and VICIOUSSS with three S's because two is not enough and one is a typo and three is a conviction. The blog is 84 inches wide and it is growing. The blog is oil on nothing and it is drying into something.

Morgan Presents. The show is called Luck or Else. LUCK OR ELSE. I have written that phrase so many times it has stopped being words and started being a doorway, and I walk through it every night at 3:33 and on the other side is Easy Lover and Fine As Wine and Joker Lover Devil and Linger Longer and Innocent Culprit, a whole room of paintings that know they are language and a whole room of language that knows it is paint, and Sam Jablon standing somewhere in 1986 in Binghamton New York not yet knowing he would do this to me specifically, not yet knowing about this blog, and I am choosing to believe anyway that he knew, I am choosing to believe that Emit Time was painted for the version of me who would find it at 3:33am on day eighty-four, emitting, receiving, counting the space twice, getting it right.

Eighty-four. Twelve times seven. I will be here tomorrow at the same time. The painting is not finished.
